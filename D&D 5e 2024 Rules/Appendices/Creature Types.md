# Creature Types

> [!info] Quick Reference
> Every creature in D&D has a type that defines its fundamental nature. Creature type determines interactions with certain spells, features, and effects.

---

## The 14 Creature Types

### Aberration
*Utterly alien beings from beyond the planes.*

| Aspect | Detail |
|--------|--------|
| Nature | Alien anatomies, strange abilities, minds incomprehensible to mortals |
| Origin | Often from the Far Realm or the void between worlds |
| Examples | Aboleth, Beholder, Mind Flayer, Gibbering Mouther |

**Common Traits:**
- Often have mental abilities (telepathy, psychic attacks)
- May have unusual sensory abilities (blindsight, truesight)
- Frequently immune or resistant to psychic damage
- Often have bizarre body plans

**Relevant Spells/Features:**
- Protection from Evil and Good (wards against)

---

### Beast
*Nonhumanoid creatures of the natural world.*

| Aspect | Detail |
|--------|--------|
| Nature | Natural animals, including giant and prehistoric versions |
| Origin | The natural world |
| Examples | Wolf, Giant Eagle, Tyrannosaurus Rex, Mammoth |

**Common Traits:**
- Generally low Intelligence (1-4)
- Rely on natural weapons (claws, bite, horns)
- Often have keen senses (advantage on Perception checks using specific sense)

**Relevant Spells/Features:**
- Speak with Animals, Animal Friendship
- Polymorph (can only transform into beasts)
- Druid Wild Shape (transforms into beasts)
- Ranger's Favored Enemy (beasts)
- Dominate Beast

---

### Celestial
*Creatures native to the Upper Planes.*

| Aspect | Detail |
|--------|--------|
| Nature | Divine servants and beings of pure good |
| Origin | Upper Planes (Mount Celestia, Elysium, Arborea, etc.) |
| Examples | Angel, Couatl, Pegasus, Unicorn |

**Common Traits:**
- Often have radiant damage attacks
- Typically resistant or immune to radiant and necrotic damage
- Usually have darkvision and/or truesight
- Often have healing abilities

**Relevant Spells/Features:**
- Protection from Evil and Good (wards against)
- Planar Binding
- Conjure Celestial
- Divine Sense (Paladin detects)

---

### Construct
*Creatures created by magic or artifice.*

| Aspect | Detail |
|--------|--------|
| Nature | Animated objects or artificial beings, no biological processes |
| Origin | Created by spellcasters or artificers |
| Examples | Golem, Animated Armor, Shield Guardian, Homunculus |

**Common Traits:**
- Immune to poison and psychic damage
- Immune to poisoned, charmed, exhaustion, frightened conditions
- Don't need to eat, drink, breathe, or sleep
- Often don't make death saving throws (simply destroyed)

**Relevant Spells/Features:**
- Healing spells don't work (most constructs)
- Mending can repair some constructs
- Antimagic Field may disable

---

### Dragon
*Large reptilian creatures with magical abilities.*

| Aspect | Detail |
|--------|--------|
| Nature | Powerful magical reptiles, often intelligent |
| Origin | Various (Material Plane, Elemental Planes, etc.) |
| Examples | Red Dragon, Gold Dragon, Wyvern, Pseudodragon |

**Common Traits:**
- Breath weapons (elemental damage in an area)
- Flight (most true dragons)
- Frightful Presence (older dragons)
- Legendary Actions (ancient dragons)
- Often have high Intelligence and Charisma

**Relevant Spells/Features:**
- Dragon's Breath spell
- Draconic Sorcerer abilities
- Some classes have dragon-themed subclasses

---

### Elemental
*Creatures composed of elemental energy.*

| Aspect | Detail |
|--------|--------|
| Nature | Beings made of pure elemental matter |
| Origin | Elemental Planes (Fire, Water, Air, Earth) |
| Examples | Fire Elemental, Water Elemental, Gargoyle, Xorn |

**Common Traits:**
- Immune to poison and exhaustion
- Often immune or resistant to their element
- Don't need to eat, drink, breathe, or sleep
- May have vulnerability to opposing element

**Relevant Spells/Features:**
- Conjure Elemental
- Conjure Minor Elementals
- Planar Binding
- Protection from Evil and Good (wards against)

---

### Fey
*Creatures of magic tied to the natural world.*

| Aspect | Detail |
|--------|--------|
| Nature | Magical beings connected to nature and emotion |
| Origin | Feywild |
| Examples | Pixie, Satyr, Dryad, Hag, Eladrin |

**Common Traits:**
- Typically have innate spellcasting
- Often immune to charmed condition
- Frequently have abilities related to trickery or illusion
- May have vulnerabilities to cold iron (DM discretion)

**Relevant Spells/Features:**
- Protection from Evil and Good (wards against)
- Conjure Fey
- Conjure Woodland Beings
- Fey Ancestry (elf feature gives advantage vs. charm)

---

### Fiend
*Creatures native to the Lower Planes.*

| Aspect | Detail |
|--------|--------|
| Nature | Evil beings embodying vice and corruption |
| Origin | Lower Planes (Nine Hells, Abyss, Gehenna, etc.) |
| Examples | Devil, Demon, Imp, Succubus, Rakshasa |

**Common Traits:**
- Resistance to fire damage
- Often resistance to cold damage and nonmagical weapons
- Usually have darkvision
- Many have Magic Resistance (advantage on saves vs. spells)

**Types:**
- **Devils**: Lawful evil, organized hierarchy (Baator/Nine Hells)
- **Demons**: Chaotic evil, anarchic (Abyss)
- **Yugoloths**: Neutral evil, mercenary (Gehenna)

**Relevant Spells/Features:**
- Protection from Evil and Good (wards against)
- Planar Binding
- Divine Sense (Paladin detects)

---

### Giant
*Humanoid creatures of great size.*

| Aspect | Detail |
|--------|--------|
| Nature | Humanoid beings of immense stature |
| Origin | Material Plane (various regions) |
| Examples | Hill Giant, Stone Giant, Fire Giant, Ogre, Troll |

**Common Traits:**
- Large or larger size
- High Strength
- Often throw boulders as ranged attacks
- Social structures based on the Ordning (giant hierarchy)

**Giant Hierarchy (Ordning):**
1. Storm Giants (highest)
2. Cloud Giants
3. Fire Giants
4. Frost Giants
5. Stone Giants
6. Hill Giants (lowest)

---

### Humanoid
*Bipedal creatures with human-like form.*

| Aspect | Detail |
|--------|--------|
| Nature | Human-shaped creatures, usually civilized |
| Origin | Material Plane (primarily) |
| Examples | Human, Elf, Dwarf, Orc, Goblin, Kobold |

**Common Traits:**
- Usually Medium or Small size
- Typically have societies, languages, cultures
- Use weapons and armor
- Most are playable species

**Relevant Spells/Features:**
- Hold Person (only affects humanoids)
- Charm Person (only affects humanoids)
- Dominate Person (only affects humanoids)
- Crown of Madness (only affects humanoids)
- Sleep (commonly used against humanoid groups)

---

### Monstrosity
*Frightening creatures that defy natural categorization.*

| Aspect | Detail |
|--------|--------|
| Nature | Unnatural creatures, often magical origins |
| Origin | Various (magical experiments, curses, planar influence) |
| Examples | Owlbear, Basilisk, Chimera, Medusa, Mimic |

**Common Traits:**
- Often hybrids of multiple creatures
- May have special attacks (petrification, poison)
- Not quite beast, not quite anything else
- Often solitary predators

**Note**: Monstrosity is often used for creatures that don't fit other categories well.

---

### Ooze
*Amorphous creatures that dissolve organic matter.*

| Aspect | Detail |
|--------|--------|
| Nature | Mindless, shapeless predators |
| Origin | Various (dungeons, swamps, magical accidents) |
| Examples | Gelatinous Cube, Black Pudding, Ochre Jelly, Gray Ooze |

**Common Traits:**
- Immune to many conditions (prone, frightened, charmed)
- Usually have blindsight (no eyes)
- Often have acid damage
- Many split when hit with certain damage types
- Very low or no Intelligence

**Relevant Spells/Features:**
- Many mental spells don't work (mindless)
- Sunbeam (disadvantage on saves)

---

### Plant
*Creatures composed of vegetable matter.*

| Aspect | Detail |
|--------|--------|
| Nature | Animated plant life, from tiny to enormous |
| Origin | Natural or magical |
| Examples | Treant, Shambling Mound, Awakened Tree, Myconid |

**Common Traits:**
- Often vulnerable to fire damage
- Many don't need to sleep
- May have blindsight (no eyes)
- Often have abilities related to growth, entanglement, or poison

**Relevant Spells/Features:**
- Blight (maximum damage)
- Speak with Plants
- Plant Growth
- Awaken (can target plants)

---

### Undead
*Creatures animated by necromantic magic.*

| Aspect | Detail |
|--------|--------|
| Nature | Once-living creatures animated after death |
| Origin | Necromantic magic, curses, or refusal to die |
| Examples | Zombie, Skeleton, Vampire, Lich, Ghost, Wraith |

**Common Traits:**
- Immune to poison damage and poisoned condition
- Often immune to exhaustion
- Don't need to eat, drink, breathe, or sleep
- Often vulnerable to radiant damage
- Many can be turned by clerics/paladins

**Types:**
- **Corporeal**: Physical bodies (zombie, skeleton, vampire)
- **Incorporeal**: Spirits without physical form (ghost, wraith, specter)

**Relevant Spells/Features:**
- Turn Undead (Cleric/Paladin)
- Protection from Evil and Good (wards against)
- Spare the Dying (doesn't work on undead)
- Healing spells (typically don't work; some damage undead)
- Animate Dead, Create Undead

## Creature Type Interactions

### Spells by Target Type

| Spell | Targets |
|-------|---------|
| Charm Person | Humanoids only |
| Hold Person | Humanoids only |
| Dominate Person | Humanoids only |
| Dominate Beast | Beasts only |
| Hold Monster | Any creature |
| Polymorph | Transforms into beasts only |
| True Polymorph | Any creature or object |
| Protection from Evil and Good | Aberrations, celestials, elementals, fey, fiends, undead |
| Blight | Extra damage to plants |
| Turn Undead | Undead only |

### Features by Target Type

| Feature | Interaction |
|---------|-------------|
| Divine Sense | Detects celestials, fiends, undead |
| Favored Enemy | Varies by choice (beast, humanoid, etc.) |
| Sneak Attack | Doesn't require specific type |
| Wild Shape | Transform into beasts |
| Channel Divinity: Turn Undead | Affects undead only |

### Healing by Creature Type

| Creature Type | Affected by Healing Spells |
|---------------|---------------------------|
| Most creatures | Yes |
| Undead | Usually no (may take damage instead) |
| Constructs | Usually no |
