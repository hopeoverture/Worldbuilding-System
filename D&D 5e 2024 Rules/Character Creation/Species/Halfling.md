# Halfling

> [!info] Quick Reference
> Halflings are small, nimble humanoids known for their luck and bravery. They can reroll natural 1s and have advantage against being frightened.

---

## Basic Traits

| Trait | Value |
|-------|-------|
| **Creature Type** | Humanoid |
| **Size** | Small (about 3 feet tall) |
| **Speed** | 30 feet |
| **Lifespan** | ~150 years |

---

## Racial Traits

### Brave
You have **Advantage** on saving throws to avoid or end the **Frightened** condition.

### Halfling Nimbleness
You can move through the space of any creature that is a size larger than you (Medium or larger).

### Lucky
When you roll a **1** on a d20 for a D20 Test, you can reroll the die, and you must use the new roll.

### Naturally Stealthy
You can take the **Hide** action even when you are obscured only by a creature that is at least one size larger than you (Medium or larger).

---

## Trait Analysis

### Lucky
One of the most consistently useful traits in the game:
- Rerolls natural 1s on all d20 tests (attacks, saves, checks)
- Natural 1s occur on 5% of rolls
- Eliminates critical misses on attack rolls
- Turns worst-case scenarios into second chances

**Math Impact**: Effectively improves your average d20 roll by about 0.5, and more importantly removes the auto-fail situations.

### Brave
- Frightened is a debilitating condition
- Many monsters cause fear (dragons, undead, fiends)
- Advantage makes it much easier to resist or break free

### Halfling Nimbleness
- Move through any Medium+ creature's space
- Excellent for repositioning in combat
- Escape grapples by simply walking away (if you can move)
- Ignore allies blocking your path

### Naturally Stealthy
- Hide behind Medium+ creatures (including allies!)
- Allows hiding in combat without full cover
- Synergizes with Rogue's Sneak Attack
- Can maintain stealth while party fights openly

## Roleplaying a Halfling

### Physical Appearance
- About 3 feet tall
- Often slightly rotund or well-fed appearance
- Typically have curly hair
- Bare, leathery feet

### Personality Considerations
- Often cheerful and optimistic
- May value comfort and good food
- Surprisingly brave when protecting friends
- Could be curious about the wider world

### Background Ideas
- Left comfortable home for adventure
- Traveling merchant or performer
- Refugee from a destroyed halfling village
- Spy using small size and stealth for infiltration

### Cultural Notes
- Often live in pastoral communities
- Value family, food, and comfort
- Generally peaceful but fierce when threatened
- Famous for luck and good fortune

## Combat Tips

- **Naturally Stealthy**: Hide behind your fighter, then emerge to attack with advantage
- **Halfling Nimbleness**: Walk right through enemy lines to reach back-row targets

- **Brave** lets you engage fearsome enemies your allies might flee from

## Optimization Notes

### Best Builds
- **Rogue**: Possibly the best species for Rogues; constant hiding and Lucky protection
- **Any martial**: Lucky eliminating natural 1s on attacks is significant
- **Casters with attack spells**: Lucky applies to spell attack rolls too

### Multiclass Considerations
- Halfling Rogues make excellent dips for any class wanting Expertise
- Lucky benefits any build equally

### Lucky vs. Other Defensive Traits
Lucky is passive and always active, affecting:
- Attack rolls (no more critical misses)
- Saving throws (better minimum results)
- Ability checks (skill floors raised)
