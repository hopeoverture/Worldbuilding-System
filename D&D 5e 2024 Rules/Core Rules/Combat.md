# Combat

> [!info] Quick Reference
> Combat is played in rounds. Each round represents 6 seconds of game time. Every participant takes one turn per round in initiative order.

## Combat Sequence

1. **Determine Surprise** (if applicable)
2. **Establish Positions** - DM describes locations
3. **Roll Initiative** - Determines turn order
4. **Take Turns** - In initiative order
5. **Repeat** - New round begins after everyone has acted

---

## Initiative

At the start of combat, each participant rolls initiative:
**d20 + Dexterity modifier**

- Higher totals act first
- Ties: DM decides for monsters; players decide among themselves
- Initiative order remains the same each round unless something changes it

### Surprise

If one side is unaware of the other:
- Surprised creatures can't move, take actions, bonus actions, or reactions on their first turn
- A creature stops being surprised at the end of its first turn

---

## Your Turn

On your turn, you can:
- **Move** up to your Speed
- Take one **Action**
- Take one **Bonus Action** (if available)
- **Free Object Interaction** (one per turn)

Movement and actions can be taken in any order and can be broken up.

### Movement

- Move up to your Speed in feet
- Can break up movement around actions
- Moving through difficult terrain costs 2 feet per 1 foot moved
- Can move through ally spaces (but not stop there unless smaller)
- Can't willingly move through enemy spaces (unless 2+ sizes different)

### Dropping Prone / Standing Up

- Drop prone: No cost
- Stand up: Costs movement equal to half your Speed
- Crawling: Each foot costs 1 extra foot of movement

## Making Attacks

### Attack Roll
**d20 + Ability Modifier + Proficiency Bonus (if proficient)**

Compare to target's **Armor Class (AC)**:
- Equal or higher = Hit
- Lower = Miss

### Ability Modifiers for Attacks

| Attack Type | Ability |
|------------|---------|
| Melee weapons | Strength (or DEX if Finesse) |
| Ranged weapons | Dexterity |
| Thrown weapons | Strength (or DEX if Finesse) |
| Spell attacks | Spellcasting ability |

### Damage Roll

On a hit: Roll the weapon's damage dice + ability modifier
- Same ability used for the attack roll
- Bonus damage from features adds to this

### Critical Hits

Natural 20 on the attack roll:
- Automatic hit
- Roll all damage dice twice, then add modifiers

### Critical Miss

Natural 1 on the attack roll:
- Automatic miss regardless of modifiers

## Melee Attacks

### Reach
Most melee weapons have 5-foot reach. Some have 10-foot reach (e.g., glaive, halberd).

### Unarmed Strikes
- Always available
- 1 + Strength modifier bludgeoning damage
- You're always proficient

### Two-Weapon Fighting
When you take the Attack action with a Light melee weapon:
- Use Bonus Action to attack with a different Light weapon in your other hand
- Don't add ability modifier to damage (unless negative or you have the fighting style)

### Grappling
Instead of an attack, you can grapple a creature:
- Target must be no more than one size larger
- Make Strength (Athletics) check vs. target's Strength (Athletics) or Dexterity (Acrobatics)
- Success: Target has the Grappled condition
- Moving a grappled creature: Your speed is halved

### Shoving
Instead of an attack, you can shove a creature:
- Target must be no more than one size larger
- Make Strength (Athletics) check vs. target's Strength (Athletics) or Dexterity (Acrobatics)
- Success: Push target 5 feet OR knock it Prone

---

## Ranged Attacks

### Range
Weapons have normal and long range (e.g., shortbow 80/320):
- Within normal range: Attack normally
- Beyond normal range (up to long range): Attack with Disadvantage
- Beyond long range: Can't hit

### Ranged Attacks in Close Combat
When hostile creature is within 5 feet and can see you:
- Ranged attack rolls have **Disadvantage**
- Applies to ranged weapons and ranged spell attacks

## Cover

| Cover Type | Bonus |
|-----------|-------|
| Half Cover | +2 AC and DEX saves |
| Three-Quarters Cover | +5 AC and DEX saves |
| Total Cover | Can't be directly targeted |

- Half: Obstacle blocks at least half the target
- Three-Quarters: Obstacle blocks about three-quarters
- Total: Completely concealed

## Damage and Hit Points

### Taking Damage
Subtract damage from current hit points.

### Dropping to 0 Hit Points
- If damage equals or exceeds remaining HP: Fall Unconscious and make Death Saving Throws
- If damage exceeds remaining HP + max HP: Instant death

### Death Saving Throws
At start of your turn while at 0 HP:
- Roll d20 (no modifiers)
- 10 or higher: Success
- 9 or lower: Failure
- Natural 20: Regain 1 HP and consciousness
- Natural 1: Counts as two failures

**3 Successes**: Stabilized (unconscious but no longer dying)
**3 Failures**: Death

### Stabilizing
- Use an action and succeed on DC 10 Wisdom (Medicine) check

## Mounted Combat

### Mounting/Dismounting
- Costs movement equal to half your speed
- Must be within 5 feet of the mount

### Controlling a Mount
- Mount acts on your initiative
- It can only Dash, Disengage, or Dodge
- Mount moves as you direct

### If Mount is Knocked Prone
- You can use reaction to land on your feet
- Otherwise you fall prone within 5 feet of mount

---

## Underwater Combat

- Melee weapons: Disadvantage unless weapon has Thrown property or is a dagger, javelin, shortsword, spear, or trident
- Ranged weapons: Automatically miss beyond normal range; Disadvantage within normal range (unless crossbow, net, or thrown weapon)
- Creatures fully submerged have Resistance to fire damage
