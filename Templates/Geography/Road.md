### Art Style

### Guidelines
When generating AI art for this road, include:
- **Path character:** The road's construction, condition, and how it relates to terrain
- **Journey sense:** The feeling of travel, of origin and destination, of distance
- **Traveler presence:** Who uses this road and what their passage looks like
- **Terrain relationship:** How the road adapts to landscape—switchbacks, bridges, cuts through hills
- **Way markers:** Milestones, signposts, shrines, the marks of maintained infrastructure
- **Danger or safety:** Whether this road feels protected or threatens ambush

### Road Integration (Roads Are Stories of Passage)
Every element should suggest travel, connection, and the meeting of peoples:
- *Don't write:* "road through forest" → *Write:* "a corridor of packed earth and crushed stone cutting through the ancient trees, the canopy closing overhead in places to create tunnels of green shadow, the road surface darker where the sun never reaches to dry it"
- *Don't write:* "worn path" → *Write:* "stones polished smooth by ten thousand passing feet, wheel ruts worn inches deep into the living rock, the central strip standing slightly higher where countless hooves have packed the earth—this road remembers everyone who has ever traveled it"
- *Don't write:* "travelers on road" → *Write:* "a merchant's wagon pulled by patient oxen, the driver dozing; pilgrims walking in a group for safety; a mounted courier racing past everyone, mud flying from his horse's hooves—the eternal traffic of a living road"

### Distinctive Feature Ideas for Roads
- **Construction evidence:** Roman-style paving, rammed earth, gravel over logs, the engineering choices of builders
- **Maintenance signs:** Fresh repairs here, neglect there, the difference between well-patrolled and abandoned sections
- **Traveler variety:** Merchants, pilgrims, soldiers, refugees, animals driven to market, each with distinctive passage marks
- **Way-markers:** Milestones with distances, shrines to travel gods, warning signs, crossroads markers
- **Danger indicators:** the places where forest presses close, isolated stretches, ruins of the unprepared
