## Image Prompts

### Art Style
**Hyper-realistic digital art, photorealistic rendering, cinematic lighting, rich textures, intricate detail on military elements and combat scenes, dramatic atmosphere, depth of field, volumetric lighting, 8K quality, masterful composition.**

### Guidelines
When generating AI art for this battle, include:
- **Tactical reality:** The terrain, formations, and maneuvers that determine outcomes—battles are won and lost for understandable reasons
- **Human scale within mass violence:** Individual combats, recognizable faces, personal moments within the larger carnage
- **Factional distinction:** Heraldry, equipment, and formation styles that identify sides without labels
- **Terrain as character:** The hill, river, wall, or weather that shaped the fighting
- **Time of day:** Light conditions that affect combat—dawn surprises, noon exhaustion, dusk last stands
- **The turning point:** The moment where advantage shifts, where one side breaks or rallies

### Battle Integration (Battles Are Decisions Made in Blood)
Every element should express the combat's character, its tactics, and its human cost:
- *Don't write:* "cavalry charge" → *Write:* "the moment the heavy cavalry struck the enemy flank—horses released from waiting, lances lowering as one, the infantry trying to turn to face a direction they never expected, the dust rising behind the charge marking the speed of their coming"
- *Don't write:* "shield wall" → *Write:* "the shield wall that held for six hours—the men in the front rank visible over their shields, exhaustion and determination mixed, the dead at their feet showing how many have fallen to keep this line intact"
- *Don't write:* "siege assault" → *Write:* "the escalade at the breach—ladders against crumbling stone, defenders pouring fire and stones on the climbers, the first attackers over the rubble dying but their bodies becoming cover for those who follow"

### Distinctive Feature Ideas for Battles
- **Formation geometry:** The shapes of organized combat—lines, wedges, squares, and the moments they break
- **Weapon diversity:** The tools of killing appropriate to era and culture—swords, spears, bows, magic, siege engines
- **Command visibility:** Standards, elevated positions, and messengers connecting leaders to forces
- **Casualty reality:** The wounded, dying, and dead in proportions that tell the battle's story
- **Environmental participation:** Rain, mud, fire, and magical effects shaping combat
- **Morale visible:** The difference between men fighting to win and men fighting to survive
