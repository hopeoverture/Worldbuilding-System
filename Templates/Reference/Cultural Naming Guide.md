# Cultural Naming Guide

This reference provides naming conventions for various real-world cultural inspirations. Use these patterns to create consistent, evocative names for characters, places, and organizations in your worldbuilding.

> [!info] Usage
> Mix and modify these patterns to create unique fantasy names. Combine syllables from different cultures, alter spellings, or blend multiple traditions to suit your world's aesthetic.

---

## European Traditions

### Germanic/Nordic
**Character Names:**
- Male: Bjorn, Erik, Gunnar, Harald, Magnus, Olaf, Ragnar, Sigurd, Thorin, Ulfric
- Female: Astrid, Freya, Helga, Ingrid, Sigrid, Brunhild, Thyra, Gudrun
- Suffixes: -son, -dottir (patronymics), -heim, -gard

**Place Names:**
- Prefixes: Frost-, Storm-, Iron-, Raven-, Wolf-
- Suffixes: -heim (home), -gard (enclosure), -fjord, -fell (mountain), -vik (bay)
- Examples: Ironheim, Ravengard, Stormfjord, Wolfsfell

**Organizations:**
- The [Animal] Brotherhood, The [Metal] Guard, The [Element] Covenant
- Examples: The Raven Brotherhood, The Iron Guard, The Storm Covenant

### Celtic/Gaelic
**Character Names:**
- Male: Aidan, Brennan, Cormac, Declan, Finn, Liam, Niall, Oisin, Ronan, Sean
- Female: Aisling, Brigid, Caoimhe, Deirdre, Fiona, Maeve, Niamh, Saoirse, Siobhan
- Prefixes: Mac- (son of), O'- (descendant of)

**Place Names:**
- Prefixes: Dun- (fort), Kil- (church), Bally- (town), Glen- (valley), Loch- (lake)
- Suffixes: -more (great), -beg (small), -rath (ringfort)
- Examples: Dunmore, Kilbride, Ballymore, Glenrath

**Organizations:**
- The Order of the [Sacred Object], Clan [Animal], The [Color] Circle
- Examples: The Order of the Rowan, Clan Stag, The Silver Circle

### Slavic/Eastern European

- Female: Anastasia, Ekaterina, Irina, Mila, Nadia, Olga, Svetlana, Tatiana, Yelena

**Place Names:**

**Organizations:**

## Asian Traditions

### Chinese-Inspired
**Character Names:**
- Structure: [Family Name] [Given Name] (family name first)
- Family Names: Chen, Huang, Li, Liu, Wang, Wu, Zhang, Zhou
- Male Given: Feng (wind), Long (dragon), Wei (greatness), Xian (immortal)
- Female Given: Hua (flower), Mei (beautiful), Xiu (elegant), Ying (cherry blossom)

**Place Names:**
- Elements: Shan (mountain), He/Jiang (river), Hu (lake), Hai (sea), Lin (forest)
- Directions: Bei (north), Nan (south), Dong (east), Xi (west)
- Examples: Longshan (Dragon Mountain), Beihu (North Lake), Donglin (East Forest)

**Organizations:**
- The [Number] [Animal] Society, The House of [Virtue], The [Element] Order
- Examples: The Nine Dragon Society, The House of Harmony, The Jade Order

## Creating Hybrid Names

### Blending Techniques

1. **Syllable Swapping:** Take the first syllable from one culture, ending from another
   - Nordic "Thor-" + Celtic "-more" = Thormore
   - Japanese "Kuro-" + Germanic "-heim" = Kuroheim

2. **Sound Shifting:** Modify sounds to feel more fantastical
   - "Vladimir" → "Valdimir" → "Valdimyr"
   - "Sakura" → "Sekura" → "Sekhari"

3. **Meaning Translation:** Use the meaning in a different linguistic style
   - "Iron Mountain" → "Ironsberg" (Germanic) or "Tessanyama" (Japanese-ish)

4. **Prefix/Suffix Mixing:**
   - Celtic "Dun-" + Slavic "-gorod" = Dungorod
   - Arabic "Al-" + Norse "-heim" = Alheim

### Consistency Tips

1. **Pick 1-2 cultures per region** - Mix too many and names feel random
2. **Consistent phonemes** - If a culture uses "th" sounds, use them throughout
3. **Similar name lengths** - Nordic names: 2-3 syllables; Japanese: 3-4 syllables
4. **Recurring elements** - If rivers end in "-flow," keep that consistent
