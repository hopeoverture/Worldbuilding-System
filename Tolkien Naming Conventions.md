# Tolkien's Naming Conventions

A comprehensive guide to how J.R.R. Tolkien named characters, places, and entities across Middle-earth, organized by race and language.

---

## Core Philosophy

Tolkien, a professional philologist, approached naming with linguistic rigor. His names are never arbitrary—they reflect:

1. **Character or Nature** - Names describe the entity's essential qualities
2. **Historical Context** - Names reflect the cultural background and era
3. **Linguistic Realism** - Languages evolve, and names show this evolution
4. **Multiple Perspectives** - The same place/person often has different names in different languages

---

## Languages Overview

| Language | Inspiration | Primary Users | Characteristics |
|----------|-------------|---------------|-----------------|
| **Quenya** | Finnish, Latin | High Elves (ceremonial) | Vowel-heavy, melodic, often ends in vowels |
| **Sindarin** | Welsh | Grey Elves, Gondorians | Consonant clusters, mutations, often ends in consonants |
| **Khuzdul** | Hebrew, Semitic | Dwarves (secret) | Triconsonantal roots, guttural sounds |
| **Adûnaic** | Semitic | Númenóreans | Royal and noble names |
| **Westron** | → English | Common Speech | "Translated" to English by Tolkien |
| **Black Speech** | Hurrian (possibly) | Mordor, Orcs | Harsh, agglutinative, no letter 'e' |
| **Rohirric** | Old English | Rohirrim | Anglo-Saxon words and structures |

## Elven Names

### Quenya Names
High Elvish, used ceremonially and for royal names in Gondor.

**Characteristics:**
- Almost always end in a vowel (-a, -ë, -o, -i)
- B, D, G only appear in clusters (MB, ND, NG)
- Uses K, Q, and X
- Melodic and flowing

**Common Elements:**
| Element | Meaning | Examples |
|---------|---------|----------|
| *-iel/-ien* | daughter/maiden | Galadriel, Lúthien |
| *-ion* | son of | Fingolfin → "Finwë's son" |
| *-ndil/-ndur* | friend/servant | Elendil ("Star-lover"), Eärendur |
| *ar-/tar-* | noble/royal/king | Ar-Pharazôn, Tar-Minyatur |
| *el-/elen-* | star | Elendil, Elenion |
| *fin-* | hair | Finwë, Fingolfin |
| *ëa-* | sea | Eärendil |

**Examples:**
- **Artanis** → "Noble woman" (Galadriel's father-name)
- **Varda** → "Sublime, exalted, lofty"
- **Eldar** → "Connected with the stars"

### Sindarin Names
The everyday Elvish language of Middle-earth, used for most names.

**Characteristics:**
- Often ends in consonants
- Never ends in -ë
- B, D, G can appear anywhere
- Does not use K, Q, or X
- Features consonant mutations (like Welsh)

**Common Elements:**
| Element | Meaning | Examples |
|---------|---------|----------|
| *-iel* | daughter | Tauriel |
| *-ion* | son | Thranduil-ion |
| *-dir/-dil* | friend | Barahir |
| *-gor/-gon* | lord/valor | Fingon, Turgon |
| *-las* | leaf | Legolas ("Greenleaf") |
| *-duin* | river | Baranduin, Anduin |
| *-ost/-gost* | fortress | Osgiliath, Minas Tirith |
| *taur-* | forest | Tauriel ("Forest maiden") |
| *celeb-* | silver | Celeborn ("Silver-tall") |

**Sindarin vs. Quenya Equivalents:**
| Quenya | Sindarin | Meaning |
|--------|----------|---------|
| Findekáno | Fingon | "Hair-commander" |
| Ondolindë | Gondolin | "Hidden rock" |
| Endórë | Endor | "Middle-land" |
| Alatáriel | Galadriel | "Maiden crowned with radiance" |

### Elvish Naming Customs
Elves typically receive multiple names:
1. **Father-name** (ataressë) - Given at birth by the father
2. **Mother-name** (amilessë) - Given later, often prophetic
3. **Chosen name** (epessë) - Self-chosen or earned title
4. **Sindarized name** - After Thingol's ban on Quenya

## Dwarf Names

### Outer Names (Public)
Dwarves use "outer names" from the language of peoples they live among—never their true Khuzdul names.

**Source:** Almost all dwarf names in *The Hobbit* come from the Old Norse poem *Völuspá* (the "Dvergatal" or "Catalogue of Dwarves").

**Examples from Norse:**
| Name | Meaning | Notes |
|------|---------|-------|
| **Thorin** | "Bold one" | From Old Norse |
| **Durin** | "Sleepy" | First of the Seven Fathers |
| **Gimli** | "Shelter from fire" | Icelandic origin |
| **Bombur** | "The swollen one" | Describes his physique |
| **Gandalf** | "Staff-elf" | Originally meant for a dwarf! |

### Inner Names (Secret)
True Khuzdul names are never revealed to outsiders—not even carved on tombs.

**Khuzdul Structure:**
- Based on triconsonantal roots (like Hebrew)
- Vowels determine case and tense
- Example root **FLK** (chisel/hew):
  - *felak* = chisel (object)
  - *afluk* = to chisel (verb)
  - *uflak* = chiseller (person)
  - *iflêk* = act of chiseling

**Known Khuzdul Words:**
| Word | Meaning |
|------|---------|
| *Khazad* | Dwarves |
| *Khazâd-dûm* | Dwarf-mansion (Moria) |
| *Baraz* | Red |
| *Zirak* | Silver |
| *Baruk* | Axes |

### Epithets (Descriptive Titles)
Dwarves earn descriptive titles translated to Common Speech:
- **Oakenshield** (Thorin) - From a battle deed
- **Ironfoot** (Dáin)
- **Stonehelm** (Thorin III)

## Hobbit Names

Tolkien "translated" Westron hobbit names into English equivalents.

### First Names
**Pattern:** Archaic or rustic English names, often flower/plant-based for women.

| Westron | "Translation" | Notes |
|---------|---------------|-------|
| *Bilba* | Bilbo | Possibly from "bil" (sword) + "beorht" (bright) |
| *Banazîr* | Samwise | Means "half-wise" |
| *Razanur* | Peregrin | From Latin "peregrinus" (wanderer) |
| *Kalimac* | Meriadoc | - |

**Common Female Names:** Rose, Lily, Daisy, Primula, Lobelia, Eglantine

### Surnames (Family Names)
Old English or rustic English style, often describing:
- **Occupations:** Gamgee (cotton wool maker)
- **Physical traits:** Proudfoot, Bracegirdle
- **Locations:** Baggins (from "bag end"), Took (Tûk)
- **Nature:** Brandybuck, Burrows

| Westron | English | Meaning |
|---------|---------|---------|
| *Labingi* | Baggins | From "bag" (cul-de-sac) |
| *Tûk* | Took | - |
| *Bophîn* | Boffin | - |
| *Galbasi* | Gamgee | "Gamwich" → cotton village |

### Place Names in the Shire
English toponymy—real or plausible English place-names:

| Westron | English | Meaning |
|---------|---------|---------|
| *Labin-nec* | Bag End | "Bag-end" (cul-de-sac) |
| *Sûza* | The Shire | - |
| *Branda-nîn* | Brandywine | "Border-water" (later punned to "Bralda-hîm" = "heady ale") |

## Men of the West (Dúnedain)

### Númenórean Names

**Quenya Royal Names (Tar-):**
Used by faithful kings, prefix *Tar-* means "King/Queen"
- Tar-Minyatur, Tar-Elendil, Tar-Ancalimë

**Adûnaic Royal Names (Ar-):**
Used by later, rebellious kings
- Ar-Pharazôn, Ar-Adûnakhôr, Ar-Gimilzôr

### Gondorian Names
Predominantly Sindarin, often borrowing famous Elvish names:
- **Ecthelion** (an elf-lord of Gondolin)
- **Denethor** (an elf-king of the Laiquendi)
- **Mablung** (a march-warden of Doriath)

**Royal Name Elements:**
| Element | Meaning | Examples |
|---------|---------|----------|
| *Ar-/ara-* | Noble, royal | Aragorn, Arathorn, Arvedui |
| *-gorn* | Valor, dread | Aragorn ("Revered king") |
| *-mir* | Jewel | Boromir, Faramir |
| *-dil* | Friend, lover | Elendil ("Star-lover") |

**Note:** Names beginning with *Ar-* are reserved for royalty among the Dúnedain.

## Rohirrim Names

Tolkien used Old English (Anglo-Saxon) for Rohan, reflecting their culture as noble horse-lords.

**Common Elements:**
| Element | Meaning | Examples |
|---------|---------|----------|
| *éo-* | horse | Éomer, Éowyn, Éothéod |
| *théod* | people, nation | Théoden, Éothéod |
| *-mund* | protection | Grimbold |
| *-helm* | helmet | Helm, Dernhelm |
| *-wine* | friend | Elfwine |
| *-gar* | spear | Déagol |

**Examples:**
- **Théoden** → "Lord of the people"
- **Éowyn** → "Horse-joy" or "lover of horses"
- **Éomer** → "Horse-famous"

**Place Names:**
- **Edoras** → Old English "courts, dwellings"
- **Meduseld** → "Mead-hall"
- **The Mark** → Germanic word for "borderland"
- **Emnet** → Actual Norfolk place-name meaning "plains"

## Evil Names

### Mordor and Black Speech

**Characteristics:**
- Harsh, guttural sounds
- Agglutinative (words built by adding pieces)
- No letter 'e'
- Long â and û common

**Common Elements:**
| Word | Meaning |
|------|---------|
| *nazg* | ring |
| *gûl* | wraith |
| *búrz* | dark |
| *lug* | tower/fortress |
| *ghâsh* | fire |
| *uruk* | orc |
| *snaga* | slave |
| *hai* | folk, race |

**Examples:**
- **Nazgûl** → "Ring-wraith" (*nazg* + *gûl*)
- **Lugbúrz** → "Dark Tower" (Barad-dûr)
- **Uruk-hai** → "Orc-folk"
- **Olog-hai** → "Troll-folk"

### Orc Names
Orcs have no true language—they cobble together corrupted words from other tongues.

**Known Orc Names:** Azog, Bolg, Gorbag, Shagrat, Grishnákh, Uglúk

**Pattern:** Harsh consonants, guttural sounds, often -g, -k, -z endings

## Place Name Patterns

### Dual Naming
Many places have multiple names reflecting different cultures:

| Common/Translated | Elvish | Meaning |
|-------------------|--------|---------|
| Rivendell | Imladris | "Deep valley of the cleft" |
| Mirkwood | Taur-e-Ndaedelos | "Forest of great fear" |
| Lonely Mountain | Erebor | "Lonely mountain" (Sindarin) |
| Moria | Khazad-dûm | "Black pit" vs. "Dwarf-mansion" |

### Structural Patterns in Elvish

**GENERIC + SPECIFIC** (two words):
- *Hithaeglir* → "Line of Misty Peaks"

**SPECIFIC + GENERIC** (compound):
- *Barad-dûr* → "Dark Tower"
- *Minas Tirith* → "Tower of Guard"

### Common Geographic Elements

| Element | Meaning | Examples |
|---------|---------|----------|
| *-duin* | river | Anduin, Baranduin |
| *-ost/-gost* | fortress/city | Osgiliath |
| *minas-* | tower | Minas Tirith, Minas Morgul |
| *barad-* | tower | Barad-dûr |
| *ered-* | mountains | Ered Luin, Ered Lithui |
| *-lin/-lind* | song/singing | Gondolin, Laurelin |
| *-dor* | land | Mordor, Gondor, Eriador |
| *-ien* | land (region) | Lórien, Ithilien |

## Practical Guidelines for Writers

### Creating Elvish-Style Names
1. **Quenya:** End in vowels, use flowing sounds, include -iel, -ion, -ndil
2. **Sindarin:** Allow consonant endings, use -dir, -gon, -duin, -ost

### Creating Dwarf-Style Names
1. Use harsh consonants: K, Z, TH, KH
2. Borrow from Norse mythology for "outer names"
3. Use descriptive epithets in Common Speech

### Creating Hobbit-Style Names
1. Use archaic English first names
2. Surnames from occupations, traits, or places
3. Flower names for women
4. Keep it rustic and grounded

### Creating Mannish Names
1. **Gondor:** Borrow Sindarin elements and famous elf names
2. **Rohan:** Use Old English roots, especially éo- (horse)
3. **Númenor:** Quenya (Tar-) or Adûnaic (Ar-) prefixes for royalty

### Creating Evil Names
1. Harsh consonants: G, K, Z, GH, SH
2. Avoid soft sounds
3. No letter 'e' for pure Black Speech
4. Compound aggressive elements
