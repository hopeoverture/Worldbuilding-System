# Father Aldric the Elder

## Overview
Father Aldric the Elder is a living relic—a man who has crowned three kings and buried two of them. At ninety-one years old, his body is a withered husk animated by a mind still sharp as cathedral glass. As High Priest of the Grand Cathedral in [[Eldoria]], he controls something every claimant to the throne desperately needs: religious legitimacy. He intends to crown a fourth king before he dies, and his choice could decide the fate of the realm.

## Appearance

### Physical Description
Aldric the Elder is ancient beyond easy reckoning—ninety-one years have reduced his body to paper-thin skin stretched over fragile bones. He stands barely five feet tall now, stooped by age until he seems to fold in on himself. His face is a map of wrinkles, deep furrows carved by decades of prayer and political maneuvering, with sunken cheeks and a jaw that has grown too sharp without the flesh of youth. But his eyes—his eyes are the grey of storm clouds and bright with an intelligence that has outlasted three monarchs. They miss nothing, these eyes, even as his body fails him.

### Attire
The High Priest wears the ceremonial white robes of his office, heavy with gold embroidery depicting the symbols of the Three—the Warden's scales, the Shepherd's crook, the Chronicler's tome. The robes seem to wear him now rather than the reverse, hanging on his diminished frame. He carries a staff not as symbol but necessity, its silver head depicting intertwined hands. Around his neck hangs the Triad Medallion, worn by every High Priest for six centuries, its weight seemingly too much for his thin neck.

## Personality

### Traits
1. He speaks in riddles and scripture, making listeners work to understand his meaning—a habit that conceals how much he's truly saying
2. He never forgets a slight, a kindness, or a confession—ninety-one years of accumulated knowledge stored in that ancient skull

### Ideals
**Sacred Trust:** "The crown is not for those who want it. It is for those the Three choose to bear its weight. My task is merely to recognize Their choice."

### Bonds
He has served the Church of the Divine Light for seventy-three years and considers it his true family. The institution must survive, even if the realm does not.

### Flaws
His certainty in his own judgment has calcified into arrogance. He believes his long experience makes him uniquely qualified to determine the realm's fate—and he may be willing to make terrible choices for what he considers the greater good.

### Demeanor
Father Aldric presents the face of benevolent senility to those who underestimate him—a useful mask that conceals razor-sharp calculation. Those who meet his eyes know better. He speaks softly, forcing others to lean close and listen carefully, and every word is chosen with the precision of a surgeon's blade.

- Available at any hour for matters of genuine spiritual crisis (he sleeps little)

## Background

### Origin
**Background:** Acolyte (entered the church at age eight)

**Occupation:** High Priest of [[Eldoria]]

**Path:** Born to a minor noble family, Aldric was given to the church as a younger son with no prospects. He discovered a vocation—and an aptitude for the politics that surround faith. Rising through the hierarchy over six decades, he was elected High Priest at fifty-seven, chosen specifically because the other candidates were too ambitious and he seemed safely pious.

### History with Protagonists
Father Aldric observes newcomers to [[Eldoria]] with interest. Adventurers unattached to factions are potential tools—or potential threats. He may approach them through intermediaries, testing their character before revealing himself.

### Current Situation
The High Priest spends his days mediating between factions who all want his endorsement, praying for guidance that doesn't come, and wrestling with a secret that grows heavier by the day. He knows that King [[High King Aldric II|Aldric II]] had a legitimized child kept hidden—and he fears what will happen when this becomes known.

## Story Role

### Purpose
Father Aldric represents the moral ambiguity of power—he genuinely wants what's best for the realm but has spent so long in political maneuvering that he may have lost sight of genuine faith. He's a source of quests, information, and uncomfortable questions about whether the ends justify the means.

### Arc
His arc centers on the secret of the hidden heir. He must decide whether to reveal what he knows (risking chaos), continue hiding it (prolonging the crisis), or try to manage the revelation himself (playing god with the realm's future).

### Stakes
If Father Aldric dies, the church's neutrality dies with him. His likely successors are either faction-aligned or lack his skill at maintaining independence. The religious legitimacy all claimants need would be thrown into chaos.

### Complications
- He knows King Aldric II had a legitimized child but not where this heir is hidden
- His health is failing faster than he admits
- His former student [[High Confessor Maren]] preaches increasingly disturbing doctrine
- The secret passage between Cathedral and Palace makes him vulnerable to the wrong discovery

## Secrets

1. He knows that King [[High King Aldric II|Aldric II]] legitimized a secret child before his death—a potential heir with a claim stronger than [[Edric Valdren]]'s. He doesn't know where this heir is hidden, only that they exist. Revealing this would throw the succession into chaos; hiding it feels like betraying his duty.

2. The entity that has been contacting the Castellan has also appeared in his dreams. It offers the location of the hidden heir in exchange for something the High Priest fears to give—access to the sealed catacombs beneath the Cathedral, where something older than the Faith was bound centuries ago.

3. He suspects [[High Confessor Maren]]'s visions are not from the Three but from something else entirely. He fears what his former student is becoming—and what he himself might have to do about it.

## Notes
Father Aldric is designed to embody the moral complexity of religious authority during political crisis. He's neither villain nor saint but a man trying to do right with imperfect information and imperfect faith. His secret about the hidden heir is a major campaign revelation that should be earned through roleplay rather than dice rolls.
