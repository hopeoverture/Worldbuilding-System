# Lady Whisper

## Overview
Lady Whisper is a ghost in [[Eldoria]]'s shadows—a masked woman who trades in secrets, selling information to all factions while belonging to none. No one knows her true face or real name; her allegiance is to profit and the exquisite power of knowing what others would kill to learn. In a city of competing spymasters, she stands alone, trusted by no one and employed by everyone, the one neutral broker in a war of whispers.

## Appearance

### Physical Description
No one has seen Lady Whisper's face—at least, no one who's lived to describe it. She's tall for a woman, moving with the fluid grace of a dancer or a duelist, her every motion deliberate and economical. Her figure is slender, her hands elegant, her voice a low contralto that could be anyone's voice or no one's. The mask she wears is simple white porcelain, expressionless, with painted red lips and empty eye holes that somehow seem to watch from impossible angles. Her age is impossible to guess; her manner suggests experience, but her movements are young.

### Attire
Lady Whisper dresses in deep purple and black, expensive fabrics cut for ease of movement. Her gown conceals practical clothing beneath—she can flee, fight, or fade into shadows without changing outfits. She wears gloves always, soft leather that leaves no fingerprints. A thin silver chain around her neck disappears into her bodice; what hangs from it is unknown. She carries no visible weapons, which means she carries invisible ones.

## Personality

### Traits
1. She never answers a direct question directly—information is currency, and she spends it deliberately
2. She remembers everything she's told, everything she's seen, and keeps meticulous records in a code no one has cracked

### Ideals
**Information Is Power:** "Gold can be spent. Armies can be broken. Secrets? Secrets are forever. The right secret in the right ear can topple kingdoms."

### Bonds
She is loyal only to herself and her network. Any apparent loyalty is merely long-term investment.

### Flaws
She has made herself indispensable to so many factions that removing her has become impossible—but this also means she has enemies who would rejoice at her death. One day, the knife will come that she doesn't see.

### Demeanor
Lady Whisper is unfailingly polite, even playful, treating the deadly game of espionage as entertainment. She finds amusement in others' desperation and takes professional pride in the elegance of well-timed revelations. Behind the mask, her tone suggests a smile—but also reminds you that smiles can be predatory.

## Role

**Secondary:** Quest-giver (for the right price)

- Blackmail material on countless nobles and merchants

### Availability
- Operates from the Noble Quarter's shadows, appearing by invitation only
- Contact through dead drops at specific locations (changed weekly)
- Will arrange meetings for those who can pay her attention fee
- Never appears in the same place twice in the same month

## Background

### Origin
**Background:** Unknown

**Occupation:** Information Broker

**Path:** Lady Whisper's history is exactly that—whispers. Some say she was a noble's daughter who discovered her family's secrets and parlayed them into power. Others claim she was a courtesan who learned that pillow talk was worth more than gold. A few believe she's not one person but several, a network wearing the same mask. She encourages all theories; mystery is its own protection.

### History with Protagonists
Adventurers are useful to Lady Whisper—they move through places her regular agents can't, they're not connected to factions, and they're often too naive to realize how much they're revealing. She may approach them as a client, as a seller of information, or as someone testing whether they're worth cultivating.

### Current Situation
The succession crisis has made Lady Whisper richer and busier than ever. Every faction wants to know what the others are planning; every noble wants to know who's plotting against them; every criminal wants to know when the Watch is coming. She's playing more games simultaneously than ever before—and the stakes have never been higher.

### Combat Role
Lady Whisper doesn't fight—she escapes, negotiates, or ensures someone else fights for her. If forced into combat, she uses poison and paralysis to disable threats, then flees. Her security is information: anyone who kills her knows their secrets die with her, and others will avenge her for the bounty of secrets she's left behind.

## Story Role

### Purpose
Lady Whisper embodies the shadows of [[Eldoria]]—the unseen wars fought with secrets and lies. She's a source of plot hooks, revelations, and moral ambiguity. Players can never be sure if they're using her or being used.

### Arc
Her arc involves the increasing danger of her position. As factions grow desperate, her neutrality becomes harder to maintain. She may be forced to choose sides, reveal herself, or take actions that compromise her carefully cultivated independence.

### Stakes
If Lady Whisper falls, the balance of information in [[Eldoria]] collapses. The secrets she's been keeping would spill out—her dead man's switches ensure that. The resulting chaos could topple houses, start wars, or expose truths that destroy lives.

### Complications
- Someone is hunting for her true identity
- Her dead man's switches make her assassination catastrophically dangerous
- Multiple factions are pressuring her to choose sides
- She may know the location of the hidden heir—and be waiting for the right bidder

## Roleplay Notes

### Voice & Mannerisms
- Speaks in a low, melodious contralto, never raising her voice
- Tilts her head like a curious bird when listening
- Never uses names—only titles, descriptions, or pronouns
- Laughs softly at things that aren't funny
- Fingers drum silently on surfaces when she's thinking

### Topics of Interest
- The psychology of secrets—why people keep them, reveal them, betray them
- The current political situation (she loves discussing what she already knows)
- Philosophy of information and power
- The foolishness of trusting anyone

### Conversation Hooks
- "You've seen something interesting. I can tell. Would you like to profit from it?"
- "Everyone has secrets. The question is merely the price."
- "I know why you're here. The real question is—do you know why I agreed to meet?"

### Sample Dialogue

> "I don't sell lies. Lies are cheap and traceable. I sell truths—carefully selected, precisely timed, devastatingly accurate truths."

> "Kill me if you like. My death triggers certain revelations. Your masters' masters would not approve."

> "Trust? How quaint. I offer something better: mutual assured destruction. Much more reliable."

## Secrets

1. She knows the secret [[Father Aldric the Elder]] carries—that King [[High King Aldric II|Aldric II]] had a legitimized heir—and she knows more than he does about where that heir is hidden. She's waiting to see which faction will pay the most for this information.

2. She was responsible for the elimination of the previous Watch captain. He discovered something about her network that made him too dangerous to live. [[Captain Helena Stern]] is getting close to discovering the same thing.

3. Her true identity: she is the disgraced daughter of a noble house, presumed dead in a fire fifteen years ago. If this becomes known, everything she's built collapses—and old enemies resurface.

## Notes

She works best when players are never quite sure of her agenda, never fully trust her information, but keep coming back because she's the only one who knows what they need to know.
