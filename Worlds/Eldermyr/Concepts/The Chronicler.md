# The Chronicler

> [!info] Generation Instructions
> The Chronicler is one of the Three equal gods of Eldermyr, representing divine memory, death, and knowledge. The Chronicler does not judge—only remembers. Every deed, every thought, every secret is inscribed. The current crisis—dead rising, souls denied rest—is a direct violation of the Chronicler's covenant.

## Overview
The Chronicler is the most mysterious of the Three—shrouded, veiled, silent, holding the Great Book in which all things are recorded. Where the Warden establishes order and the Shepherd nurtures life, the Chronicler ensures that nothing is forgotten and that all souls find their final rest. The Chronicler does not judge; judgment is the Warden's domain. The Chronicler only *remembers*—every deed, every word, every secret thought inscribed in pages beyond number. When mortals die, the Chronicler receives them, shows them their complete lives, and grants them eternal peace. The current horror at [[The Ashen Fields]]—the dead rising, souls trapped, the fundamental promise of rest violated—strikes at the heart of the Chronicler's covenant. The faithful are terrified, for if the Chronicler cannot guarantee rest, what becomes of death itself?

## Divine Nature

### Appearance
The Chronicler manifests as a completely veiled figure—gender unknowable, features invisible, identity obscured beneath layer upon layer of grey shrouds. Only the hands are visible: pale, long-fingered, turning the pages of an immense book that seems to contain infinite text in scripts no mortal can read. The Chronicler's presence brings silence—not the absence of sound, but the presence of listening. In that silence, one becomes aware of how much they have forgotten, how much they have tried to forget, how much they never knew they remembered. The book the Chronicler holds is different to each observer: some see their own name on its pages; some see the names of those they've lost; some see nothing but endless, patient text recording everything that has ever happened.

### Personality
The Chronicler does not comfort or condemn. The Chronicler simply *is*—the eternal witness, the patient recorder, the keeper of everything that was. Where the Warden's justice is cold and the Shepherd's mercy is warm, the Chronicler's neutrality is neither. The dead receive their Recording without praise or blame; they see their lives complete and understand the weight of their choices. This is not punishment—it is clarity. For some, the Recording brings peace; for others, it is harder to bear than any torment. The Chronicler offers neither forgiveness nor condemnation, only truth—and the promise that after truth comes rest.

## Worship

### Worshippers
Who worships this deity?
- The grieving seeking comfort that their dead have found rest
- Scholars, historians, and archivists
- Those approaching death seeking assurance
- Anyone who wishes to remember the dead
- Necromancers (perversely—they fear the Chronicler most)
- Librarians and record-keepers

### Clergy
**Titles:** Chronicler-Priest, Rememberer, Voice of the Silent; High Rememberer (regional leaders); the Silence (Synod representative)
**Requirements:** Training in funerary rites; ordination through the Synod; vows of truth (Chronicler-priests may not lie, ever)
**Vestments:** Grey robes with deep hoods; veils covering the lower face; silver chains bearing the book symbol
**Duties:** Conducting funerals and last rites; maintaining records of the dead; comforting the grieving; guarding graves; opposing necromancy; preserving historical records

### Temples
Describe typical temple:
- **Architecture:** Quiet, contemplative structures; libraries attached to crypts; often built into or beside cemeteries
- **Layout:** Central hall of remembrance with records of local dead; library wing; crypt or ossuary; meditation chambers
- **Sacred objects:** The Book of the Dead (local record of every soul who has passed); memorial candles; ancestor tablets; sacred quills
- **Location preferences:** Near cemeteries; quiet districts; anywhere memory needs keeping

### Rituals
**Daily Observances:**
- Dawn recitation of the names of those who died this day in history
- Midday meditation on mortality
- Evening recording of the day's events (personal and communal)

**Major Rituals:**
1. **The Last Rites:** Sacrament ensuring the soul's passage to the Chronicler's halls; failure to perform risks the soul being lost
2. **The Reading of Names:** Remembrance Night ritual where every name in the Book of the Dead is spoken aloud
3. **The Sealing:** Ritual closing of graves to prevent disturbance; now desperately important at [[The Ashen Fields]]

### Prayers
Common prayers or invocations:
- "Chronicler, receive this soul. Write their name in your book. Grant them rest."
- "May the dead be remembered. May the living remember. May all find rest in time."
- "In silence, truth. In memory, presence. In death, peace."

### Offerings
Preferred offerings:
- Written memories of the deceased
- Candles lit in remembrance
- Time spent in silence and contemplation

### Commandments
Divine mandates for followers:
1. Ensure the dead receive proper rites—no soul should wander
2. Preserve truth; the Chronicler's record must not be falsified
3. Remember those who have passed; they live on in memory
4. Oppose those who disturb the dead or deny them rest
5. Seek knowledge and preserve it for those who come after

### Sins
What actions offend this deity?
- Necromancy—disturbing the dead, denying them rest
- Destroying records or erasing history
- Lying about the dead
- Preventing souls from receiving last rites
- Forgetting the departed deliberately

## Mythology

### Origin
The Chronicler emerged at creation as one of the Three who spoke the First Words. While the Warden spoke Order and the Shepherd spoke Life, the Chronicler spoke Memory—ensuring that what was made would not pass into nothing, that existence would have meaning because it would be remembered. Without Memory, the Warden's Order would be purposeless and the Shepherd's Life would be fleeting. Scripture does not explain where the Three came from, only that they simply *are*.

### Major Myths
List 2-3 stories featuring this deity:

1. **The Covenant of Rest:** When the first mortals died, their souls wandered in confusion, unsure where to go. The Chronicler descended, showed them their lives in full, and offered them peace. In return, the living would honor the dead and ensure all souls received proper rites. This is the covenant—and it is breaking.
2. **The Burnt Library:** When a tyrant ordered the great library destroyed to erase his crimes from history, the Chronicler appeared among the flames and removed a single book—containing everything the tyrant had done. The library burned, but the truth survived.
3. **The Unwritten Page:** The Chronicler's book has empty pages at its end. Prophecy says these will be filled only when the world ends, or transforms into something new. Some heretics believe mortals can write on those pages—changing the future instead of merely recording the past.

### Prophecies
Prophecies involving this deity:
- "When the dead refuse to rest, the covenant shall be tested. When the covenant breaks, the Silence shall be broken."
- "The last page will be written by one who remembers what was forgotten."

### Legendary Deeds
Famous divine actions:
1. Speaking Memory at the creation of the world
2. Establishing the Covenant of Rest with the first dead
3. Recording the First Words themselves, preserving creation's echo

## History

### Rise to Power
The Chronicler has always existed as one of the Three—there was no rise, only eternal presence. The Chronicler's domain was established at creation itself.

### Major Events
1. **The Covenant of Rest:** Establishment of the afterlife and the promise of peace
2. **The Thornhall Schism:** When the Chronicler's Silence tradition nearly split from the Orthodox Synod; reconciled but northern practices remain distinct
3. **[[The Battle of Ashen Fields]]:** 10,000 souls trapped, the covenant violated on a scale never before seen

### Current Era
The Chronicler's covenant is broken, and no one knows why—or how to repair it. The dead rising at [[The Ashen Fields]] are souls who should be at rest but are instead trapped, forced to fight battles that ended twenty-three years ago. Chronicler-priests work alongside [[The Grey Wardens]] to lay the risen to rest, but for every one they free, more rise. The priests' prayers for guidance receive only silence. The Book in [[Thornhall]]—a sacred replica meant to remain blank—has begun writing itself. The monks who read what appears have not spoken since. The Chronicler's Silence—the Northern church faction—interprets this crisis as divine testing; others fear the Chronicler has been somehow compromised. The truth may be worse: the Chronicler may be as helpless as mortals against what [[The Unnamed]] has done.

## Secrets

1. **The Chronicler cannot free the souls at [[The Ashen Fields]].** Something has intercepted them—[[The Unnamed]]—and the Chronicler's power cannot reach them. This has never happened before.

2. **The self-writing Book contains prophecy.** The monks who read it saw what is coming—and their silence is not shock but preparation. They are writing something themselves, in response.

3. **The Chronicler may have recorded [[The Unnamed]]'s true name.** If that name could be found in the Great Book—or its replica—it might provide power over the ancient horror. But finding it would require reading what cannot be read.

4. **[[High Confessor Maren]]'s visions are not from the Chronicler.** She believes she receives divine truth, but Chronicler-priests who have studied her prophecies find them *wrong* in subtle ways—history misremembered, names altered, as if whatever speaks to her doesn't have access to the true record.

## Plot Hooks

1. **The Speaking Silence:** The monks at the Silent Monastery have begun to speak—but only one word each, and the words together seem to form a message. The party must collect the words and decode what the Chronicler is trying to say.

2. **The Stolen Name:** Someone has found a way to remove a name from the Book of the Dead—effectively erasing a soul from existence. The party must discover who and why before more names vanish.

3. **The Last Rites:** A noble dying of poison begs the party to ensure their soul receives proper rites. But someone powerful wants them to die unblessed, their soul left to wander—as leverage, or punishment.

4. **The Ancestor's Memory:** A Chronicler-priest can contact a specific ancestor who might know how [[The Mad King]] first contacted [[The Unnamed]]. But the ritual requires items from the ancestor's life—and their descendants don't want the truth revealed.
