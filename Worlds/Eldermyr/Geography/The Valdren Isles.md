# The Valdren Isles

## Overview
The Valdren Isles are a crescent-shaped archipelago of seven major islands and dozens of smaller islets curving through [[The Trader's Sea]] like a golden necklace. For three centuries, [[House Valdren]] has ruled these islands as their private domain, building a mercantile empire that rivals kingdoms. The islands' harbors shelter the mightiest trading fleet in Eldermyr, while their banks hold secrets and treasures from across the known world. In the succession crisis, the Valdren Isles have become the unassailable stronghold from which the house projects power toward the mainland, their ships blockading rivals while their gold finances rebellion.

### Social Structure
Island society is rigidly stratified: House Valdren and allied merchant families at the top, ship captains and traders in the middle, fishermen and laborers at the bottom. Wealth determines everything—birth matters less than gold. The banking families of Goldport wield power second only to House Valdren itself.

### Isolation Effects
Islanders consider themselves superior to "dustwalkers" (mainlanders). They've developed distinct dialects, customs, and a fierce independence. The sea is in their blood—even noble children learn to sail. Islanders trust their own kind and view outsiders with profitable suspicion.

## History

### Geological History
The Valdren Isles are the peaks of a drowned mountain range, separated from the mainland when sea levels rose in ancient times. The islands' rocky bones are the same stone as the distant [[The Shepherd's Teeth|Shepherd's Teeth]] hills on the mainland.

### Discovery
The islands have been known since before written history. Ancient peoples used them as fishing camps and refuges.

### Settlement
Three centuries ago, the Valdren family—then merely wealthy merchants—purchased exclusive rights to the islands from a cash-strapped crown. They transformed a backwater into an empire.

### Colonial History
The Valdrens never colonized the islands; they *became* the islands. The family's identity is inseparable from their island domain. They've since expanded influence to mainland coastal towns.

## Cultural Significance

### Island Culture
Valdren Islanders have developed a distinct identity: fiercely mercantile, deeply superstitious about the sea, and convinced of their superiority over mainlanders. They measure worth in gold, judge character by business dealings, and consider a handshake as binding as any contract—breaking one means social death.

### Traditions
Unique customs:
- Ship-naming ceremonies (elaborate; involve blood sacrifice to the sea)
- The Settling of Accounts (annual debt reconciliation; festival and terror combined)
- First Trade (children's coming-of-age involves profitable transaction)
- Sea burials (only criminals buried on land)

### Religion
- The New Faith has a presence but is subordinate to sea traditions
- Sailors follow the Old Ways' sea spirits openly
- The Golden God (informal) — Wealth itself is nearly worshipped
- Temple of Fortune in Corvana (syncretistic; blesses ventures)

### Legends
Stories about the islands:
- The islands are a drowned kingdom; ruins lie beneath the waves
- The Valdren family made a pact with sea spirits for success
- Exile's Rock was once a sorcerer's tower
- The Golden Harbor was blessed by a sea god

### Names

- The Merchant Isles (trading partners)
- The Gold Chain (poetic)
- The Pirate Nest (enemies, unfairly)
