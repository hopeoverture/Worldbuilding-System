# Echoes of the First Shadow

> [!info] Generation Instructions
> When generating an adventure, create a complete quest with compelling narrative, balanced encounters, meaningful choices, and satisfying resolution. Consider the hook that draws players in, the obstacles they must overcome, the NPCs they'll meet, and the rewards they'll earn. Adventures should have clear objectives while allowing for player agency and creative solutions.

## Overview
The Inner Shield has fallen. The four Elemental Lodestones lie shattered, and the path to the heart of [[The Obsidian Fortress]] stands open. But the heroes are too late to stop the ritual entirely. High Priestess Vala, wielding the terrible [[Book of Endings]], has completed the final incantation. The [[Shackled God]] cannot fully escape his prison, but he has projected his consciousness through the breach, incarnating as the **Avatar of the First Shadow**. Now, on a platform of Architect-stone floating above the yawning void between worlds, the party must face the Cult's ultimate champion and then the god itself. The world's fate hangs on a single battle. There will be no retreat, no reinforcements, no second chances. This is the end of the Age of Shadows and the beginning of something else, whether that be salvation or annihilation depends entirely on what happens in the next hour.

## The Hook

### How It Begins
The Obsidian Fortress trembles as the party defeats Warden Thrax. Through the control room's obsidian windows, they see the sky tear open above the central spire. Purple-black lightning arcs across the heavens. Reality itself screams. A pillar of absolute darkness erupts from the fortress heart, punching through the clouds. They have perhaps minutes before whatever is happening becomes permanent. There is no time for rest, no time for preparation. They must go now.

### Quest Giver
**Name:** The World Itself
**Motivation:** Survival
**What They Offer:** Everything worth fighting for

### Initial Information
What do the players know as they rush toward the final battle:
- The ritual has begun but may not be complete, there is still a chance to disrupt it
- High Priestess Vala commands the ritual, she must be stopped first
- The Book of Endings grants Vala power beyond her natural abilities
- The Shackled God has been trying to manifest since the adventure began, the corruption in the land comes from his dreaming
- If the Avatar fully forms and breaks the Prison from within, the world ends

### Urgency
**Time Pressure:** Critical

**Consequences of Delay:** Each round the party delays, the Avatar grows stronger. If they take a long rest, the world ends. If they take more than an hour, the Avatar becomes permanent. There is no "later."

## Background

### The Situation
High Priestess Vala has succeeded where a thousand years of Covenant effort failed. Using the [[Book of Endings]], a grimoire written in the Shackled God's own blood during his imprisonment, she has torn a hole in the Prison's walls wide enough for her master's consciousness to pass through. The entity cannot fully escape, the seals hold that much, but it can project a fraction of its power into a physical vessel. Vala intended to be that vessel, to become a god incarnate. She will get her wish, though not as she imagined. The Avatar is not merely possession; it is transformation. The woman called Vala will cease to exist, consumed by divinity, and what emerges will be something that has not walked the world since the Age of Myth.

### History
How did this situation develop:
- The [[Covenant of the Unveiled Truth]] has worked for centuries toward this moment
- [[The Prophet of Chains]] unified the scattered faithful and identified the seal locations
- The Cult infiltrated the [[Obsidian Fortress]] and converted it into a temple
- The Book of Endings was recovered from the Sunken Vault at tremendous cost
- The party's interference at the Obsidian Gate disrupted the timeline but did not stop it
- Vala accelerated the ritual when she learned the Lodestones were destroyed

### Key NPCs' Goals
| NPC | Goal | Method |
|-----|------|--------|
| High Priestess Vala | Become the Shackled God's vessel | Complete the ritual using the Book of Endings |
| The Avatar | Break free of the Prison entirely | Destroy the heroes, then shatter the remaining seals from within reality |
| The Prophet of Chains | See the Liberation completed | He is present spiritually, watching through the void |
| The Party | Save the world | Kill Vala, banish the Avatar, reseal the breach |

### What's at Stake
**If the party succeeds:** The Avatar is banished, the breach seals, and the Prison holds for another three thousand years. The Age of Shadows ends. The Age of Heroes begins. The party becomes legends.

**If the party fails:** The Avatar stabilizes its form, breaks the remaining seals from inside reality, and the Shackled God enters the world. All existence is consumed by the Void. Every soul that ever lived or will live is unmade. The story ends, not just for the party, but for everything.

## Objectives

### Primary Objective
Defeat the Avatar of the First Shadow before it can fully manifest and break free of the Prison.

### Secondary Objectives
Optional goals that enhance the adventure:
1. Destroy the Book of Endings so it cannot be used again
2. Recover Vala's soul before the Avatar consumes it entirely (allows resurrection)
3. Close the breach cleanly rather than violently (affects epilogue)
4. Survive without any party member deaths

### Hidden Objectives
Goals the party may discover during play:
1. The Prophet of Chains is watching through the void, his consciousness tied to the Avatar. Destroying the Avatar will destroy the last fragment of what was once High Priest Malakor. The Silver Flame paladin in the party may sense this.
2. The Architect platform contains a Seal Anchor that could be activated to strengthen the Prison permanently, but doing so requires someone to stay behind and activate it manually, trapping them in the Prison forever.

### Success Conditions
How is victory defined:
- The Avatar is reduced to 0 HP in its final phase
- At least one party member survives to witness the breach closing
- The Prison reseals (happens automatically when the Avatar is destroyed)

### Failure Conditions
How can the party fail:
- Total party kill before the Avatar is destroyed
- Taking more than 10 rounds after the Avatar reaches Phase 3 (it stabilizes permanently)
- All party members are pulled into the Void during Phase 3 (automatic TPK)

## Adventure Structure

### Act 1: The High Priestess
**Goal:** Defeat the caster maintaining the ritual breach before the Avatar fully manifests.

**Key Scenes:**
1. **The Ascent:** The party races up the central spire through collapsing corridors as reality warps around them. Gravity shifts. Walls bleed shadow. This is a skill challenge (5 successes before 3 failures) using Athletics, Acrobatics, Arcana, or Perception (DC 15). Failure means 3d6 force damage from reality fluctuations.
2. **The Ritual Chamber:** The party bursts into a massive circular room open to the void-torn sky. High Priestess Vala floats at the center, wreathed in purple-black lightning, the Book of Endings hovering before her. Cultist defenders (4 Cult Fanatics, 2 Shadow Demons) move to intercept while Vala continues her chanting.

**Information to Reveal:**
- Vala is in a trance state, the ritual requires her concentration
- The Book of Endings pulses with visible power, it is the anchor for the spell
- Something massive and dark is forming in the tear above, growing more solid each round
- Disrupting Vala's concentration or destroying the Book will complete the ritual prematurely but imperfectly

**Transition to Act 2:** When Vala is reduced to half HP or the Book is destroyed, she screams in triumph and terror. The shadow above plunges downward. Vala's body convulses, tears, and reforms. She becomes the vessel.

### Act 2: The Vessel Emerges
**Goal:** Survive the transformation and prepare for the true battle.

**Key Scenes:**
1. **The Transformation:** Vala's body rises into the air, shadow pouring into her from the breach above. Her screams become something inhuman. Her flesh splits and reforms. She grows, fifteen feet, twenty feet, her form becoming a humanoid shape of pure darkness with eyes of burning void-light. This takes 2 rounds, during which the party can prepare (buffs, positioning, short rests are impossible).
2. **The Floor Falls:** As the Avatar completes its formation, the chamber floor cracks and gives way. The party falls (with a DC 15 Dexterity save to land safely or take 3d6 bludgeoning damage) onto the Architect Platform, a disc of ancient white stone floating in the void between worlds. The real battle begins.

**Rising Tension:**
- During the transformation, whispers fill everyone's minds, offering power, offering peace, offering an end to pain (DC 18 Wisdom save or gain one level of exhaustion from psychic assault)
- The sky above is now pure void, studded with alien stars that are not stars
- The platform they stand on is the only solid ground, surrounded by infinite darkness

**Midpoint Twist:**
As the Avatar forms, a voice echoes through the void: [[The Prophet of Chains]], speaking from somewhere beyond. "Beautiful, is it not? The truth made flesh. Do not weep for Vala. She is free now. As you will all be free. As everything will be free. The chains are breaking."

**Transition to Act 3:** The Avatar's formation completes. It turns its void-eyes upon the party. Combat begins.

### Act 3: The God-Killer
**Goal:** Reduce the Avatar to 0 HP across all three phases before it fully stabilizes.

**Key Scenes:**
1. **Phase 1, The Warrior:** The Avatar attacks with brutal physical force, wielding a greatsword of crystallized shadow. It fights like a knight, testing the party's martial prowess.
2. **Phase 2, The Sorcerer:** At 66% HP, the Avatar discards its weapon and begins casting devastating spells, calling upon the void's power. Shadow creatures emerge from the darkness to support it.
3. **Phase 3, The Void:** At 33% HP, the Avatar attempts to drag everyone into its home plane. The platform cracks and begins falling into the void. The party fights in freefall while chunks of reality collapse around them.

**Climactic Encounter:** The Avatar of the First Shadow (full statistics below).

**Resolution Options:**
- **Victory:** The Avatar is destroyed. The breach seals violently. The platform stabilizes. The surviving party members find themselves standing in the ruins of the ritual chamber as dawn breaks.
- **Pyrrhic Victory:** The Avatar is destroyed but with the hidden objective (Seal Anchor activation). One party member sacrifices themselves to permanently strengthen the Prison.
- **Total Victory:** The Avatar is destroyed, the Book of Endings is destroyed, Vala's soul is recovered, and the party survives intact. The Age of Heroes begins with its greatest heroes still alive.

## Locations

### Overview Map
The adventure takes place in three connected areas:
1. The Central Spire (vertical ascent through warped corridors)
2. The Ritual Chamber (circular room open to the sky)
3. The Architect Platform (floating disc in the void, the true arena)

### Primary Location
**Name:** The Ritual Chamber / The Architect Platform

**Description:** The ritual chamber is a massive circular room at the spire's peak, three hundred feet in diameter with no ceiling. The walls are carved with Architect script that bleeds darkness. At the center, a ritual circle of silver and void-iron holds the breach open. When the floor collapses, it reveals the Architect Platform beneath, a disc of white stone fifty feet in diameter that floats in the space between worlds. This is the arena for the final battle.

**Key Features:**
- The Breach Above (provides dim purple light, source of shadow creatures)
- The Ritual Circle (if intact, empowers the Avatar; can be disrupted with a DC 20 Arcana check)
- The Platform Edge (no guardrails; creatures can be pushed off but the void eventually returns them, see Phase 3)
- The Seal Anchor (hidden under the platform's center; DC 25 Investigation to find)

**Encounters Here:** High Priestess Vala, Avatar of the First Shadow (all phases)

### Location 2: The Central Spire
**Description:** A vertical shaft through the fortress's heart, now twisted by the ritual's power. Gravity is unreliable. Shadows move independently of their sources. The walls weep something that might be tears.

**Key Features:**
- Reality Distortions (skill challenge hazards)
- Collapsing Architecture (DC 15 saves to avoid falling debris, 2d6 bludgeoning)
- Cultist Corpses (those who couldn't contain the power they called)

**Encounters Here:** No formal encounters, but environmental hazards and the skill challenge.

### Location 3: The Void Between Worlds
**Description:** Not a location so much as an absence of location. During Phase 3, the platform descends into this space. There is no up or down, no light except what the combatants bring, and alien stars that watch with malevolent intelligence.

**Key Features:**
- No Gravity (flight speeds become default movement)
- No Reference Points (disorienting; disadvantage on ranged attacks beyond 60 feet)
- The Watching Stars (each round, one random creature takes 2d6 psychic damage as the void's attention touches them)

**Encounters Here:** Avatar of the First Shadow (Phase 3 only)

## Key NPCs

### Allies

#### The Prophet's Last Memory
**Role:** The ghost of who Malakor once was

**Description:** During the battle, particularly if a Silver Flame paladin or cleric is present, they may sense a presence separate from the Avatar, a soul in torment watching from the void. This is the last fragment of High Priest Malakor, the man the Prophet once was, trapped within the consciousness of the Shackled God.

**Personality:** Anguished, horrified, desperate

**What They Want:** For someone to end his suffering by destroying the Avatar (and thus, him)

**What They Know:** The Seal Anchor location, Vala's true name (can weaken her momentarily if spoken)

**How They Can Help:** If contacted (Speak with Dead, or a natural 20 on a Religion check to reach through the void), the Prophet's Memory can provide: the location of the Seal Anchor, Vala's true name (speaking it during Act 1 causes her to lose concentration for one round), or a blessing (+2d6 radiant damage on the next attack against the Avatar).

### Neutrals

#### The Watching Stars
**Role:** The Void's passive observers

**Description:** Alien intelligences that exist in the space between worlds. They watch the battle with interest but do not intervene. They are older than the Shackled God and bound by laws even he does not understand.

**Personality:** Unknowable, patient, curious

**What They Want:** Entertainment, perhaps? Understanding? Their motives are alien.

**Attitude Toward Party:** Neutral. They will not help, but they will not hinder. They simply... watch.

### Antagonists

#### Primary Antagonist: High Priestess Vala
**Role:** The Ritual Anchor / Phase 0 Boss

**Description:** Vala was once a beautiful elven woman with silver hair and violet eyes, a scholar who sought forbidden knowledge and found the Covenant's promises of truth irresistible. She rose through the ranks through ruthlessness and genuine devotion, eventually earning the honor of wielding the Book of Endings. Now, standing at the culmination of her life's work, she is wreathed in shadow and crackling with void energy, her eyes already beginning to show the emptiness that will consume her. She believes, truly believes, that becoming the Avatar's vessel is apotheosis, that she will become a god. She is wrong. There will be nothing left of her.

**Personality:** Fanatical, ecstatic, utterly certain. She speaks of the coming Liberation with religious rapture. She sees the party not as enemies but as the last witnesses to her ascension.

**Motivation:** To become one with the Shackled God, to transcend mortality, to be remembered as the one who freed the True God.

**Methods:** She relies on the Book of Endings' power (granting her 9th-level spells she could not normally cast), her cult defenders, and the ritual itself (which heals her 10 HP at the start of each of her turns while she maintains concentration).

**Weakness:** Breaking her concentration (Constitution saves against damage as normal) disrupts the ritual. Destroying the Book of Endings ends her borrowed power. Speaking her true name ("Valandiel Mournsong") causes her to lose concentration for one round. She cannot move from the ritual circle's center without abandoning the ritual.

**Combat Notes:** High Priestess Vala (CR 13). See stat block below. She fights defensively, trusting her cultists to hold the line while she completes the ritual. If forced to fight directly, she uses the Book's power to cast devastating spells while the ritual sustains her.

#### Secondary Antagonist: Avatar of the First Shadow
**Role:** The Final Boss / God Incarnate

**Description:** The Avatar is not a creature in the traditional sense; it is a fragment of a god forced into physical form, a concept given flesh. It stands fifteen feet tall, a humanoid shape of absolute darkness that hurts to look upon directly. Its edges flicker and blur, as if reality itself cannot quite accept its presence. Two points of purple-white light serve as eyes, burning with an intelligence older than the world. When it moves, shadows trail behind it like smoke. When it speaks (rarely), the words form directly in the listener's mind, bypassing ears entirely. It smells of nothing, for nothing is what it is made of.

**Personality:** The Avatar is not Vala; no trace of her personality remains. It is the Shackled God's will given form, cold, ancient, and utterly certain of its purpose. It does not hate the party; it does not hate anything. It simply intends to be free, and they are in the way.

**Motivation:** Break free of the Prison. Destroy the heroes. Unmake the seals. Consume all that is.

**Methods:** In Phase 1, overwhelming physical force. In Phase 2, devastating magic and summoned reinforcements. In Phase 3, pulling everything into the void where its power is absolute.

**Weakness:** Divine magic (radiant damage) is particularly effective (+1d6 damage per die). The ritual circle, if disrupted, prevents the Avatar from healing. The Seal Anchor, if activated, deals 100 radiant damage to the Avatar. The Avatar cannot exist without the breach; if the breach were somehow closed while it still lives (impossible without the Seal Anchor), it would be destroyed instantly.

**Combat Notes:** Avatar of the First Shadow (CR 22, adjusted for party). See full stat block below.

#### Boss Encounter: Avatar of the First Shadow
**Location:** The Architect Platform

**Setup:** The floor gives way. The party falls (DC 15 DEX save or 3d6 bludgeoning) onto a floating disc of white stone in the void between worlds. The Avatar descends from above, a god incarnate, ready to destroy the last obstacle between it and freedom.

**Enemies:**
| Creature | Number | Notes |
|----------|--------|-------|
| Avatar of the First Shadow | 1 | CR 22 equivalent; 3 phases |
| Shadow Spawn (Phase 2) | 4 | Summoned; MM Shadow, p. 269 |

**Phases/Tactics:**

**Phase 1: The Warrior (100%-66% HP)**
The Avatar fights as a knight of the void, wielding a greatsword of crystallized darkness. It focuses on the strongest-looking melee combatant, seeking to prove its physical supremacy. It uses Multiattack every round, saves Legendary Actions for defensive repositioning and punishing overextension.

- Primary Attack: Void Greatsword (2 attacks, +14 to hit, 4d6+8 slashing plus 2d8 necrotic)
- Uses Chains of the Void to restrain multiple enemies
- Prioritizes killing one target before moving to the next
- If HP drops below 75%, uses Consume the Light

**Phase 2: The Sorcerer (66%-33% HP)**
At 66% HP, the Avatar hurls its sword into the void and begins casting. It summons 4 Shadows to support it and focuses on area-denial spells. It hovers 30 feet above the platform, forcing melee characters to find ways to reach it.

- Primary Attack: Void Bolt (3 bolts, automatic, 3d10 force each, choose targets)
- Summons 4 Shadows on initiative count 20 of the first round
- Uses Sphere of Annihilation (as spell, but larger) to control the battlefield
- Uses Legendary Actions for emergency teleports and Whispers of the Void

**Phase 3: The Void (33%-0% HP)**
At 33% HP, the Avatar screams in a voice that shakes reality. The platform cracks and begins falling into the void. The battle becomes three-dimensional as gravity fails. The Avatar attempts to drag everyone into the Prison where its power is absolute.

- All creatures gain a fly speed equal to their walking speed (the void has no gravity)
- The platform breaks into 1d4+2 floating chunks (10-ft radius each)
- The Avatar uses Aura of Annihilation every round (30-ft radius, 3d10 force damage, CON save half)
- The Avatar attempts to grapple and drag creatures into the deepest void (escape DC 22)
- Every round, the alien stars deal 2d6 psychic damage to one random creature
- After 10 rounds in Phase 3, the Avatar stabilizes and cannot be destroyed (party fails)

**Lair Actions/Legendary Actions:**

**Legendary Actions (3/round):**
- **Void Step (1 action):** Teleport up to 60 feet to a space in dim light or darkness.
- **Whispers of the Void (1 action):** One creature within 60 feet must succeed on a DC 20 WIS save or take 3d8 psychic damage and be unable to take reactions until the start of its next turn.
- **Chain Lash (2 actions):** Make one Chains of the Void attack.
- **Consume the Light (3 actions):** Magical darkness fills a 60-foot radius centered on the Avatar until the start of its next turn. All light sources of 3rd level or lower are extinguished.

**Lair Actions (Initiative 20):**
Phase 1-2:
- Chains erupt from the platform. All creatures must succeed on DC 18 DEX save or be restrained until they use an action to break free (DC 18 STR).
- The breach above pulses. All creatures take 2d6 necrotic damage (no save). The Avatar heals 20 HP.
- A wave of despair emanates from the Avatar. All creatures must succeed on DC 18 WIS save or be frightened until initiative 20 of the next round.

Phase 3:
- A platform chunk is pulled into the void. Creatures on it must succeed on DC 18 DEX save or fall (take 6d10 force damage from void exposure and reappear on a random remaining chunk).
- The alien stars focus on one creature. That creature has disadvantage on all saves until initiative 20 of the next round.
- Reality tears. A 20-foot line of absolute darkness appears. Creatures in the line take 8d6 necrotic damage (DEX save half).

**Victory Conditions:**
- Reduce the Avatar to 0 HP (it does not make death saves; at 0 HP, it discorporates)
- Survive until the Avatar is destroyed

**Rewards:**
- 41,000 XP (or milestone to level 12)
- The world is saved (priceless)
- The Void Shard (a fragment of the Avatar's crystallized essence; Very Rare, see below)

### Exploration Challenges

#### The Seal Anchor Discovery
**Location:** Beneath the Architect Platform's center (Phase 2-3)

**Description:** The platform is not merely a floating disc; it is a seal anchor, one of seven created by the Architects to reinforce the Prison. If activated, it will deal catastrophic damage to any being of the void (including the Avatar) and permanently strengthen the Prison's walls, but the activator must remain in physical contact with the anchor for the full activation sequence (3 rounds) and will be sealed inside the Prison when it closes.

**Skills Useful:**
- Investigation DC 25 to find the anchor beneath the platform
- Arcana DC 20 to understand how to activate it
- Religion DC 18 to understand the cost

**Hazards:**
- Accessing the anchor during combat requires being prone on the platform for a full round (provoking attacks)
- Activating the anchor while the Avatar lives provokes its focused attention

**Rewards for Thorough Exploration:**
- A way to guarantee victory (at ultimate cost)
- Understanding of the Architects' true purpose
- A choice that defines a character's legacy

### Red Herrings
Optional false leads:
1. The breach above appears closeable if enough force is applied (it is not; only the Avatar's destruction or the Seal Anchor can close it)
2. The Book of Endings might be used against the Avatar (it cannot; it only works for summoning, not banishing)

### Revelation Moments
**Twist/Revelation:** If the party contacts the Prophet's Memory, they learn that [[The Prophet of Chains]] is bound to the Avatar. Destroying the Avatar destroys the last remnant of the man Malakor once was. For Silver Flame devotees in the party, this is the final fate of one of their order's greatest priests, a man who fell trying to strengthen the seals. His death is a mercy.

**When It's Revealed:** During Phase 2 or 3, when contact with the void is strongest.

**Impact:** Provides closure for the Prophet's storyline. May cause hesitation (or renewed determination). Adds weight to the victory.

### Puzzles

#### The Seal Anchor Activation
**Location:** Beneath the Architect Platform

**Description:** The Seal Anchor is a complex mechanism of Architect design, a series of concentric rings that must be aligned while maintaining physical contact with the central crystal. The rings are inscribed with symbols that shift and change.

**Solution:** The activator must succeed on three consecutive Intelligence (Arcana) checks DC 18, 20, and 22 (one per round), while maintaining contact with the anchor. Each failed check deals 2d10 radiant damage as the mechanism rejects improper inputs.

**Hints:**
- The symbols follow a pattern based on the cardinal elements (fire, water, earth, air)
- The Prophet's Memory can reduce all DCs by 5 if consulted
- A character who has studied Architect ruins (from earlier adventures) has advantage

**Bypass Options:**
- A natural 20 on any check completes that step regardless of total
- Divine Intervention automatically succeeds
- The Wish spell can activate the anchor instantly (but still traps the caster)

### Alternative Approaches
How can the party approach this differently:
- **Stealth:** Impossible at this stage; the Avatar knows they're coming
- **Diplomacy:** The Avatar cannot be reasoned with. Vala is too far gone. The cultists welcome death.
- **Brute Force:** The intended approach, though tactical brute force
- **Deception:** A party member could pretend to convert to buy time, but the Avatar would sense the deception within one round

### Failure Modes
If things go wrong, how can the adventure continue:
- **Partial TPK:** If some party members die but one survives to destroy the Avatar, the adventure succeeds (pyrrhic victory)
- **Near TPK with retreat:** Not possible; there is no retreat from the void
- **Total failure:** The campaign ends. The Avatar stabilizes. The world is consumed. However, the DM might allow a divine intervention to reset to the start of Phase 3 (one-time cosmic mulligan)

## Aftermath

### Immediate Consequences
What happens right after the adventure concludes:

**If Successful:**
The Avatar discorporates with a sound like reality itself sighing in relief. The breach above slams shut. The void recedes. The platform rises, carrying the survivors back to the ruins of the ritual chamber. Dawn breaks through the shattered ceiling, the first true dawn in months. The Avatar is destroyed instantly as radiant light floods the void. The Prison seals permanently; the Shackled God will never threaten the world again. ## Running the Adventure

### GM Notes
Tips for running this adventure effectively:
- Emphasize the finality. This is the END. Make the players feel it.
- Track Avatar HP visibly; let players see progress
- Describe the environment constantly, the void is alien and terrifying
- Give dying characters dramatic last words
- If a player wants to sacrifice themselves for the Seal Anchor, let them have a heroic speech
- The Avatar should feel overwhelming but not unfair; it has clear patterns
- Use music: battle themes for combat, silence for the void, triumphant themes for victory

### Pacing Suggestions
- Act 1 (Ascent and Ritual Guards): 45-60 minutes
- Transition (Transformation): 15-20 minutes
- Act 2/3 (Avatar battle): 2-3 hours
- Aftermath and epilogue: 30-60 minutes

### Common Player Actions
| If Players... | Then... |
|---------------|---------|
| Try to destroy the Book of Endings immediately | It can be destroyed (AC 20, HP 50, immune to necrotic/psychic), but doing so triggers the transformation early |
| Try to flee | There is nowhere to go; the fortress is collapsing and the only exit is through victory |
| Try to reason with Vala | She is beyond reason but may hesitate for one round if her true name is spoken |
| Try to push the Avatar off the platform | It cannot be moved against its will (Legendary Resistance, immune to forced movement) |
| Try to stabilize fallen allies during Phase 3 | Possible but dangerous; the void is hostile to healing |
| Try to use the Book of Endings against the Avatar | The book only summons; it cannot banish |

### Scaling Difficulty
**For Weaker Parties:**
- Remove one phase from the Avatar (skip Phase 2)
- Reduce Avatar HP by 25%
- Have the Prophet's Memory intervene once, dealing 50 damage to the Avatar
- Allow one free short rest during the transformation
- Remove the time limit from Phase 3

**For Stronger Parties:**
- Add Phase 0: A weakened Prophet of Chains appears to test the party before they reach Vala
- Increase Avatar HP by 25%
- Add more shadow creatures in Phase 2 (6 instead of 4)
- Reduce the Phase 3 time limit to 7 rounds
- The Book of Endings has a Guardian Shade (use Wraith statistics) that defends it

### Foreshadowing for Future Adventures
- The alien stars remember. They may visit Valdoria in the future.
- The Shackled God is still imprisoned. He will never stop trying to escape.
- Other prisoners exist in the void. One of them has noticed the party.
- The Covenant's knowledge survives in scattered fragments; someone will find them.
- The Architects who built the Prison may not all be dead.

## Secrets

1. **The Seal Anchor can guarantee victory but costs a life.** The party may never discover this if they don't investigate thoroughly.

2. **The Prophet's Memory can be contacted during Phase 3.** This provides closure for Malakor's storyline and potential combat advantages.

3. **Vala's soul can be recovered with Resurrection-tier magic after the battle.** If the players think to try, she can be saved, though she'll be traumatized and face justice for her crimes.

4. **The Book of Endings was written by the Shackled God himself.** Using it too often attracts his attention, even from within the Prison.

5. **The alien stars are remnants of a previous creation, beings from before the current gods made the world.** They watch because they've seen this before, and they want to see how it ends this time.

### Avatar of the First Shadow
*Huge aberration (divine), chaotic evil*

**Armor Class** 20 (natural armor)
**Hit Points** 350 (28d12 + 168)
**Speed** 40 ft., fly 60 ft. (hover)

| STR | DEX | CON | INT | WIS | CHA |
|-----|-----|-----|-----|-----|-----|
| 26 (+8) | 16 (+3) | 22 (+6) | 20 (+5) | 18 (+4) | 24 (+7) |

**Saving Throws** Str +14, Con +12, Wis +10, Cha +13
**Skills** Perception +10, Intimidation +13
**Damage Resistances** cold, fire, lightning; bludgeoning, piercing, and slashing from nonmagical attacks
**Damage Immunities** necrotic, poison, psychic
**Damage Vulnerability** radiant (+1d6 per die of radiant damage)
**Condition Immunities** charmed, exhaustion, frightened, paralyzed, poisoned, stunned
**Senses** truesight 120 ft., passive Perception 20
**Languages** all, telepathy 120 ft.
**Challenge** 22 (41,000 XP)

**Legendary Resistance (3/Day).** If the Avatar fails a saving throw, it can choose to succeed instead.

**Magic Resistance.** The Avatar has advantage on saving throws against spells and other magical effects.

**Aura of Despair.** Any creature that starts its turn within 30 feet of the Avatar must succeed on a DC 21 Wisdom saving throw or be frightened until the start of its next turn. Creatures that succeed are immune to this aura for 24 hours.

**Phased Existence.** The Avatar's form changes as it takes damage:
- **Phase 1 (350-231 HP):** The Warrior, melee-focused
- **Phase 2 (230-116 HP):** The Sorcerer, caster-focused, summons 4 Shadows
- **Phase 3 (115-0 HP):** The Void, reality warps, time limit begins

**Actions (Phase 1)**

**Multiattack.** The Avatar makes two Void Greatsword attacks.

**Void Greatsword.** *Melee Weapon Attack:* +14 to hit, reach 15 ft., one target. *Hit:* 29 (4d6 + 8) slashing damage plus 13 (2d8) necrotic damage.

**Chains of the Void (Recharge 5-6).** Each creature within 30 feet must succeed on a DC 21 Strength saving throw or be restrained by spectral chains for 1 minute. A restrained creature takes 14 (4d6) cold damage at the start of each of its turns and can repeat the save at the end of each of its turns.

**Actions (Phase 2)**

**Void Bolt (Replaces Greatsword).** The Avatar releases three bolts of void energy. Each bolt automatically hits a creature the Avatar can see within 120 feet, dealing 16 (3d10) force damage. The Avatar can direct all bolts at one target or split them.

**Sphere of Annihilation.** The Avatar creates a 10-foot-radius sphere of darkness at a point within 90 feet. The sphere lasts until the start of the Avatar's next turn. Any creature that enters the sphere or starts its turn there takes 28 (8d6) necrotic damage.

**Actions (Phase 3)**

**Aura of Annihilation.** All creatures within 30 feet of the Avatar take 16 (3d10) force damage at the start of the Avatar's turn. Constitution saving throw DC 21 for half.

**Void Grasp.** *Melee Weapon Attack:* +14 to hit, reach 10 ft., one creature. *Hit:* The target is grappled (escape DC 22). While grappled, the creature takes 21 (6d6) necrotic damage at the start of each of its turns. The Avatar can drag the grappled creature with it when it moves.

**Legendary Actions**

The Avatar can take 3 legendary actions, choosing from the options below. Only one legendary action can be used at a time and only at the end of another creature's turn.

- **Void Step (1 Action).** The Avatar teleports up to 60 feet to a space in dim light or darkness.
- **Whispers of the Void (1 Action).** One creature within 60 feet must succeed on a DC 20 Wisdom saving throw or take 13 (3d8) psychic damage and be unable to take reactions until the start of its next turn.
- **Chain Lash (2 Actions).** The Avatar makes one Chains of the Void attack if available, or forces one creature within 60 feet to make a DC 21 Strength save or be pulled 30 feet toward the Avatar.
- **Consume the Light (3 Actions).** Magical darkness fills a 60-foot radius centered on the Avatar until the start of its next turn. All light sources of 3rd level or lower are extinguished.
