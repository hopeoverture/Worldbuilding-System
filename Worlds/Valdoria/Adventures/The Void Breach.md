# The Void Breach

> [!info] Generation Instructions
> A surreal horror interlude set immediately after the destruction of the Elemental Lodestones. Reality has fractured within the Obsidian Fortress, creating a nightmare corridor where the Far Realm bleeds through. This adventure serves as an atmospheric palate cleanser and resource-management challenge between the fortress infiltration and the final boss encounter, while introducing the cosmic horror elements that will define the finale.

## Overview
The Lodestones are destroyed. Warden Thrax lies dead. The way to the Inner Sanctum should be clear. But the protective barrier did not simply fall—it shattered. The magical energies that once held reality stable have inverted, and the corridor connecting the Control Room to the Antechamber of the Prison has become a wound in the world. Through this wound, the Far Realm bleeds. The party must traverse a hallway where geometry weeps, where gravity is a suggestion, and where things that should not exist hunt them through spaces that cannot be. At the end awaits either madness or a miraculous sanctuary—a pool of divine light where the party can recover before the final confrontation with the Avatar of the First Shadow.

## The Hook

### How It Begins
The party stands in the wreckage of the Control Room, Warden Thrax's body cooling at their feet. The door to the Inner Sanctum—their goal since entering this nightmare fortress—stands before them. As they approach, they notice the doorframe weeping black ichor. The door opens not onto a corridor but onto impossible space: a hallway that stretches to infinity one moment and compresses to arm's length the next. The walls breathe. The floor tilts at angles that hurt to perceive. And somewhere in that chaos, something with too many mouths begins to scream.

### Quest Giver
**Name:** Survival Instinct / Divine Imperative

**Motivation:** The party must reach the Prison's Antechamber before High Priestess Vala completes her ritual. There is no going back—the Lodestone chamber is collapsing behind them.

**What They Offer:** The only path forward. Behind is death by structural collapse; ahead is death by Far Realm exposure. At least ahead has a chance.

### Initial Information
What do the players learn at the start?
- The corridor should be approximately 200 feet long according to the fortress schematics they found
- Reality appears to be "broken" here—the Lodestone destruction caused magical backlash
- They can hear chanting from somewhere ahead—the ritual continues
- Time may not be passing normally; urgency is critical

### Urgency
**Time Pressure:** Critical

**Consequences of Delay:** Every round spent in the breach risks sanity loss and increases the chance of attracting Far Realm entities. Additionally, High Priestess Vala's ritual progresses—the Avatar's manifestation draws nearer with each passing moment.

## Background

### The Situation
The Elemental Lodestones served dual purposes: they powered the fortress's defenses AND stabilized the reality fabric near the Prison of Shadow. The Architects of Dawn knew that proximity to the Imprisoned would cause planar bleed, so they designed the Lodestones to anchor reality in place. When the party destroyed them (necessary to lower the Inner Shield), they inadvertently created a breach between the Material Plane and the Far Realm. The corridor connecting the Control Room to the Inner Sanctum now exists partially in multiple dimensions simultaneously. The Far Realm—that alien space beyond the known planes—has seeped through the cracks. Its native entities, drawn by the psychic beacon of the Imprisoned's stirring, now infest the breach.

### History
How did this situation develop?
- The Architects built redundancies: the Lodestones would fail gradually if destroyed, giving evacuees time to escape
- However, the Imprisoned's growing power has weakened reality's fabric over millennia
- The combination of Lodestone failure plus Prison proximity plus the entity's active dreaming created perfect conditions for a Far Realm incursion
- This is precisely why the Covenant could not simply destroy the Lodestones themselves—they needed the barrier down but the corridor passable for their ritual components

### Key NPCs' Goals
| NPC | Goal | Method |
|-----|------|--------|
| The Imprisoned | Wake fully | Dream-bleed destabilizes reality further |
| Far Realm Entities | Feed on sanity | Hunt anything with thoughts |
| Echo of the Architects | Guide worthy souls to safety | Manifest as the Pool of Light |

### What's at Stake
**If the party succeeds:** They reach the Pool of Light, recover their resources, and enter the final battle at full strength. The brief respite grants them perspective on what they face—and why it must be stopped.

**If the party fails:** They emerge from the breach exhausted, insane, or dead. Even partial success (reaching the Antechamber with heavy losses) dramatically reduces their chances against the Avatar.

## Objectives

### Primary Objective
Traverse the Void Breach corridor and reach the Antechamber of the Prison of Shadow.

### Secondary Objectives
Optional goals that enhance the adventure:
1. Reach the Pool of Light sanctuary and take a Long Rest before the finale
2. Minimize sanity loss (fewer than 3 failed saves)
3. Discover the Architect's Memory—a vision explaining the Prison's true nature

### Hidden Objectives
Goals the party may discover during play:
1. The breach contains fragments of the Imprisoned's dreams—observing them reveals its true nature and potential weaknesses
2. A soul of a long-dead Architect lingers in the Pool of Light and can grant one character the Blessing of Dawn (advantage on the first saving throw against the Avatar)

### Success Conditions
How is victory defined?
- At least one party member reaches the Antechamber with sanity intact
- (Optimal) The party rests at the Pool of Light and enters the finale at full resources

### Failure Conditions
How can the party fail?
- Total Party Kill by Far Realm entities
- All party members driven permanently insane (6+ Wisdom save failures)
- Taking so long that the Avatar manifests before they arrive (extremely unlikely unless deliberately stalling)

## Adventure Structure

### Act 1: The Geometry of Madness
**Goal:** Enter the breach and survive the initial environmental hazards while establishing the surreal tone.

**Key Scenes:**
1. **The Threshold:** The party steps through the door and reality inverts. Describe the sensory overload—colors that smell, sounds that have texture, the floor becoming the ceiling. Each character must make a DC 13 Wisdom saving throw or gain one level of the Madness condition (see Hazards).
2. **The Gravity Carousel:** The corridor rotates. "Down" changes every round (roll d6 to determine direction). Characters must make DC 14 Dexterity saving throws each time gravity shifts or fall 1d6 x 10 feet in the new "down" direction, taking appropriate falling damage.
3. **The Whispering Geometry:** The walls begin speaking—not in words, but in concepts. Characters hear their deepest fears articulated by angles that should not exist. Insight DC 15 reveals these are not attacks but the Far Realm's natural state—it communicates through existential dread.

**Information to Reveal:**
- The corruption intensifies closer to the Prison
- The Imprisoned is dreaming—they are walking through its nightmare

**Transition to Act 2:** A cluster of mouths materializes from the wall—the first Gibbering Mouther. Combat begins.

### Act 2: The Denizens of the Breach
**Goal:** Survive combat encounters with Far Realm entities while continuing to navigate environmental hazards.

**Key Scenes:**
1. **Mouths in the Walls:** Gibbering Mouthers emerge from surfaces that momentarily stabilize into flesh. The party must fight while gravity continues to shift.
2. **The Star Spawn Vanguard:** Star Spawn Manglers phase through non-space, striking from impossible angles. They don't want to kill—they want to drag victims into the Far Realm.
3. **The False Exit:** The party sees the Antechamber door—beautiful, normal, mundane. It's a trap. A Star Spawn Hulk waits beyond, positioned to ambush anyone who rushes forward without caution.

**Rising Tension:** The corridor grows darker. The whispers grow louder. Characters begin to doubt their own perceptions—is that party member real, or a Far Realm reflection?

**Midpoint Twist:** A party member (determined randomly or by lowest Wisdom) begins seeing things the others cannot—specifically, a shimmering path through the chaos. This character has gained temporary "Void Sight." The visions are terrifying but accurate; following them reveals the safest route.

**Transition to Act 3:** The character with Void Sight glimpses something impossibly beautiful ahead—a pool of pure light in the darkness. Hope.

### Act 3: The Pool of Light
**Goal:** Reach the sanctuary, recover, and prepare for the finale.

**Key Scenes:**
1. **The Final Sprint:** The party runs toward the light while Far Realm entities mass behind them. This is a chase sequence—five successful DC 14 Athletics/Acrobatics checks (cumulative party successes) before three failures, or the entities catch them.
2. **The Sanctuary:** The Pool of Light is real—an Architect failsafe, a bubble of pure reality anchored by divine magic. Within its radius, the Far Realm cannot reach. The light heals, restores, and grants a Long Rest in only 10 minutes of actual time.

**Climactic Encounter:** No combat in the sanctuary—this is the eye of the storm. Instead, the emotional weight of what comes next settles on the party. They can see through the light's edge: the Antechamber, High Priestess Vala, the gathering shadow.

**Resolution Options:**
- Rest fully and proceed
- Speak with the Architect's Echo for lore and a blessing
- Rush forward without resting (ill-advised)

## Locations

### Overview Map
The Void Breach is not mappable in traditional terms. It exists as a conceptual space between two fixed points (Control Room and Antechamber). The party experiences it as a corridor, but its actual geometry is non-Euclidean. Key "locations" are emotional/experiential waypoints rather than physical places.

### Primary Location
**Name:** The Breach Corridor

**Description:** A hallway that was once 200 feet of black obsidian and dim torchlight. Now it is a wound in space. The walls twist into Escher-like impossibilities. Through cracks in reality, alien vistas are visible—purple skies with too many suns, landscapes of living flesh, voids filled with watching eyes. The air smells of copper and burning parchment. Sound arrives before its source.

**Key Features:**
- Non-Euclidean geometry (distances are unreliable)
- Variable gravity (changes every 2-3 rounds)
- Psychic whispers (constant low-level sanity pressure)
- Reality anchors (obsidian pillars that provide brief stability when touched)
- Flesh portals (walls that become permeable, allowing creatures through)

**Encounters Here:** Gibbering Mouthers, Star Spawn Manglers

### Location 2: The False Exit
**Description:** A 30-foot section where reality appears normal—mundane obsidian walls, stable gravity, silence. It's a predator lure. The Star Spawn Hulk uses this pocket of calm to ambush prey.

**Key Features:**
- Deceptively mundane appearance
- Door visible at the far end (illusory)
- The Hulk hides in a fold of space, undetectable until it strikes
- Detect Magic reveals reality here is "too" normal—artificially so

**Encounters Here:** Star Spawn Hulk (ambush)

### Location 3: The Pool of Light
**Description:** A perfect sphere of golden radiance, 60 feet in diameter, containing breathable air, stable ground (manifested as white marble), and a shallow pool of luminous water at its center. The sanctuary exists in stark contrast to the surrounding madness—stepping inside feels like waking from a nightmare.

**Key Features:**
- Long Rest mechanics compressed to 10 minutes
- Complete protection from Far Realm influence
- The pool water heals 2d8+10 HP to any creature that drinks (once per creature)
- An echo of an Architect (ghostly female form) can appear if addressed
- The party can see the Antechamber through the light's edge, observing the ritual in progress

**Encounters Here:** None (sanctuary)

## Key NPCs

### Allies

#### The Architect's Echo
**Role:** Lore Provider / Blessing Granter

**Description:** A translucent woman in ancient robes, her form flickering like a candle in wind. She speaks in a voice that sounds like many voices harmonized. Her eyes hold millennia of sorrow and the faintest ember of hope.

**Personality:** Gentle but distant, as if speaking across an immense gulf of time. She does not fully understand the present but remembers the past with crystalline clarity.

**What They Want:** For the seals to hold. For the Imprisoned to remain imprisoned. For her people's sacrifice to have meant something.

**What They Know:**
- The Prison was built with seven bindings, each requiring a divine sacrifice
- The Imprisoned is not evil in a mortal sense—it is alien, incomprehensible, and its freedom would unmake reality's fundamental nature
- The Avatar is only a projection; destroying it does not destroy the entity, only forces it back to sleep

**How They Can Help:** Can grant one character the Blessing of Dawn (advantage on first saving throw against the Avatar). Can explain the Avatar's phases if asked directly.

### Neutrals

#### The Far Realm Itself
**Role:** Environmental Antagonist (Non-Sentient)

**Description:** The Far Realm is not a place with hostile inhabitants—it IS hostility to coherent thought. It doesn't want to hurt the party; it simply exists in a way that mortal minds cannot process.

**Personality:** N/A—the Far Realm has no personality. It is a state of being.

**What They Want:** Nothing. The Far Realm does not want. It simply is.

**Attitude Toward Party:** Indifferent malevolence—like a storm or a disease.

### Antagonists

#### Primary Antagonist: The Breach Itself
**Role:** Environmental Hazard

**Description:** The Void Breach is not a creature but a condition—a cancer in reality caused by the Lodestones' destruction near the Prison. It constantly tries to assert Far Realm physics on the Material Plane. Gravity fails. Geometry rebels. Sanity frays. The Breach is the encounter.

**Personality:** N/A

**Motivation:** Entropy—the Breach exists because reality was damaged and seeks equilibrium by spreading the damage.

**Methods:** Gravity shifts, whisper attacks, spawning portals for denizens.

**Weakness:** Reality anchors (obsidian pillars), the Pool of Light, and concentrated will (successful saving throws stabilize local space briefly).

**Combat Notes:** The Breach uses Lair Actions.

#### Secondary Antagonist: The Gibbering Collective
**Role:** Minion Swarm

**Description:** Multiple Gibbering Mouthers that have partially merged within the breach, forming a semi-connected consciousness. They share sensory input and coordinate attacks.

**Connection to Primary:** The Mouthers are native Far Realm creatures drawn by the breach's formation. They feed on sanity, which the breach makes abundant.

## Encounters

### Combat Encounters

#### Encounter 1: Mouths in the Walls
**Location:** The Breach Corridor (mid-section)

**Trigger:** Automatic upon entering the mid-section (~50 feet in)

**Enemies:**
| Creature | Number | Notes |
|----------|--------|-------|
| Gibbering Mouther | 3 | Emerge from walls one per round |

**Tactics:** The Mouthers focus fire on characters who fail gravity saves (prone/fallen). They use their Gibbering ability to compound the sanity pressure of the environment. One Mouther attempts to grapple while others attack.

**Difficulty:** Medium

**Terrain Features:**
- Shifting gravity (new direction every 2 rounds)
- Reality anchors (obsidian pillars; characters adjacent to one have advantage on saves vs. gravity shifts)
- Flesh walls (difficult terrain; movement costs double)

**Rewards:** Passage forward; sanity points preserved (if successful)

#### Encounter 2: Phase Hunters
**Location:** Between Mouther Zone and False Exit

**Trigger:** 1d4 rounds after the Mouthers are defeated

**Enemies:**
| Creature | Number | Notes |
|----------|--------|-------|
| Star Spawn Mangler | 2 | Phase in and out of material reality |

**Tactics:** Manglers attack from impossible angles (treat as invisible until they attack). They focus on spellcasters first, trying to prevent dimensional anchor or banishment spells. If reduced below half HP, they attempt to drag a victim into the Far Realm (grapple + dimensional shift).

**Difficulty:** Hard

**Terrain Features:**
- Unstable reality (Manglers have advantage on stealth)
- Dimensional folds (spaces where Manglers can hide fully)
- Whisper intensification (DC 12 Wisdom save or disadvantage on next attack)

**Rewards:** Clear path to False Exit; one Mangler drops a Void Shard (see Magic Items)

#### Boss Encounter: The Lurking Hulk
**Location:** The False Exit

**Setup:** The party sees a stable corridor and a door. It looks safe—too safe. The Star Spawn Hulk waits in a fold of space, detecting vibrations. It attacks the first creature to move more than 20 feet into the "safe" zone.

**Enemies:**
| Creature | Number | Notes |
|----------|--------|-------|
| Star Spawn Hulk | 1 | Ambush position, surprise round likely |

**Phases/Tactics:**
1. **Phase 1 (Ambush):** The Hulk bursts from folded space with a surprise round attack. It uses its Psychic Mirror ability to reflect any spell damage back at casters.
2. **Phase 2 (Rampage):** Below half HP, the Hulk enters a frenzy, gaining an extra attack but losing its damage resistance as its form becomes unstable.

**Lair Actions/Legendary Actions:** The Breach provides one Lair Action per round:
- Gravity Pulse: All creatures must succeed on DC 14 Strength save or be thrown 20 feet in a random direction.
- Whisper Scream: All creatures must succeed on DC 13 Wisdom save or take 2d6 psychic damage.
- Reality Flicker: One creature (DM's choice) must succeed on DC 15 Intelligence save or be partially displaced—their attacks have disadvantage until the start of their next turn.

**Victory Conditions:** Reduce the Hulk to 0 HP or bypass it entirely (possible with clever illusions or stealth; Hulk has blindsight but relies primarily on vibration).

**Rewards:** Path to Pool of Light; the Hulk's remains contain a Crystallized Void Fragment (worth 500 gp to collectors of extraplanar materials).

### Exploration Challenges

#### Navigating the Non-Euclidean
**Location:** Throughout the Breach

**Description:** The corridor does not follow normal geometry. Characters who try to map or logically navigate become disoriented. The party must rely on intuition (Wisdom), magical senses (Arcana), or the Void Sight granted to one character.

**Skills Useful:** Wisdom (Survival) DC 14, Intelligence (Arcana) DC 16, Wisdom (Insight) DC 15, or following the Void Sight character

**Hazards:** Taking the "wrong" path adds 1d4 x 10 feet of effective distance and triggers an additional gravity shift.

**Rewards for Thorough Exploration:** Discovering a reality anchor allows safe passage through one hazard zone; finding the Architect's memory fragment provides context for the finale.

### Puzzles

#### The Reality Anchor Sequence
**Location:** Mid-corridor

**Description:** Five obsidian pillars stand in a pentagonal arrangement. They pulse with fading light. The party must touch them in the correct order to create a stable zone for 1 minute. Incorrect sequences cause a Gravity Pulse.

**Solution:** Touch them in order of brightness (dimmest to brightest), representing the order they've been failing.

**Hints:**
- Arcana DC 14 reveals the pillars are connected to fundamental forces
- The order is inscribed in Architect script on the floor (History DC 15 to read)
- Trial and error: two wrong attempts before random damage

**Bypass Options:** Fly/levitate through the area, accepting gravity shift damage; dispel magic on the pillars (DC 17) temporarily stabilizes the zone.

## Running the Adventure

### GM Notes
Tips for running this adventure effectively:
- Emphasize sensory horror: describe impossible colors, sounds that shouldn't exist, the feeling of wrongness.
- Keep combat brisk; this is an interlude, not a dungeon. If encounters drag, have gravity shifts or whisper effects force movement.
- The Pool of Light should feel like salvation—describe its warmth, safety, and peace in stark contrast to the preceding nightmare.
- The Architect's Echo is optional but enriches the finale's emotional impact.
- Track Wisdom saves carefully; accumulating failures should feel consequential.

### Pacing Suggestions
- Act 1: 15-20 minutes (establish tone, first hazards)
- Act 2: 30-40 minutes (combat encounters, navigation)
- Act 3: 15-20 minutes (sanctuary, rest, lore, emotional beat)
- Total: 60-80 minutes

### Common Player Actions
| If Players... | Then... |
|---------------|---------|
| Try to dispel the breach | Explain it's not magical in the conventional sense; it's a wound in reality |
| Attack the walls/floor | Walls "bleed" but reform; damage is meaningless |
| Cast Banishment on entities | Works normally; excellent tactical choice |
| Try to teleport through | Blocked; the space is dimensionally anchored (ironically) |
| Fly to avoid gravity | Works until gravity shifts to a direction they can't fly against |
| Rest outside the Pool | Impossible; the breach prevents rest (auto-fail Long Rest) |

### Scaling Difficulty
**For Weaker Parties:**
- Reduce Gibbering Mouthers to 2
- Remove the Star Spawn Hulk (False Exit is just a lure with no ambush)
- Lower hazard DCs by 2
- Grant Pool of Light benefits automatically (no interaction needed)

**For Stronger Parties:**
- Add a Star Spawn Seer observing from the Far Realm (casts spells through portals)
- Increase gravity shift frequency
- The Hulk regenerates 10 HP/round while in the breach
- Time pressure: after 10 rounds, the breach begins collapsing (2d6 force damage/round)

## Secrets

1. **The Void Sight Character:** They didn't develop this ability randomly. The Imprisoned specifically reached out to them, curious about the minds approaching its prison. This is not malevolent—the entity is simply aware of them now.

2. **The Pool's Price:** The Architect's Echo maintains the sanctuary through the last of her spiritual essence. After the party leaves, she ceases to exist entirely. She knows this and considers it a worthy trade.

3. **The True Geography:** The corridor is 200 feet long. The party experienced it as much longer because the Imprisoned's dreams distort subjective time. In "real" time, only 3 minutes pass in the Material Plane—but for the party, it feels like an hour.

## Notes
- This adventure serves three purposes: (1) atmospheric bridge between action-heavy fortress and boss fight, (2) resource recovery opportunity, (3) cosmic horror tone-setter for the finale.
- The Far Realm elements distinguish this from standard "evil temple" aesthetics; the threat is alien, not demonic.
- The Pool of Light is essential for party balance; without a rest, the finale becomes nearly impossible at intended difficulty.
- The Architect's Echo is optional but adds emotional weight; DMs should encourage interaction.
- Time tracking is narrative, not literal; don't let players math out "real" time passage.
- The Void Sight mechanic gives one player special agency; rotate this if playing multiple sessions.
