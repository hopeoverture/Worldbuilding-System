# The Arcane Synod

> [!info] Character Summary
> The Arcane Synod is the primary magical authority in [[Thondorian]], combining the functions of magic academy, regulatory body, and arcane inquisition. Headquartered in the island fortress of [[Thalassar]], they train all registered mages, hunt rogue practitioners, and guard secrets that could destroy the world. Led by the centuries-old [[Archmage Vylara]], the Synod is simultaneously the greatest repository of magical knowledge and its most paranoid guardian. They are respected, feared, and resented in equal measure—a necessary evil in a world where unsupervised magic has repeatedly brought catastrophe.

## Overview

Magic is not a gift in Valdoria; it is a curse that must be controlled. This is the Synod's fundamental belief, born of bitter experience. Twice in recorded history, unregulated magic has nearly destroyed civilization—first in the Cataclysm that ended the Age of Myth, and again in the Mage Wars that followed. The Arcane Synod exists to ensure there is no third time. They dispatch "Watchers" to every major settlement to identify children showing magical talent. These children are taken—willingly or not—to the Spire of Tides, where they are trained, tested, and bound by oaths that make betrayal nearly impossible. Graduates serve the Synod for life, whether as researchers, teachers, or the dreaded Inquisitors who hunt those who practice magic without sanction. The Synod has enemies everywhere: kingdoms that resent their interference, hedge-witches who curse their name, and darker forces that would love to see the Watchers fail.

## Classification

### Type
**Academy Type:** Magic Academy / Regulatory Body / Arcane Inquisition

**Educational Focus:** The Synod teaches all schools of arcane magic with emphasis on control, restraint, and the dangers of overreach. Beyond spellcraft, they train students in magical law, history of arcane disasters, and identification of dark magic.

### Status
**Prestige Level:** Renowned (Feared)

The Synod's graduates are the most sought-after mages in the world—and the most watched. Having a Synod credential opens every door; having Synod attention closes them permanently.

**Accreditation:** The Synod is self-accrediting. No other institution has the authority—or the foolishness—to judge them.

## Location

### Setting
**Settlement:** [[Thalassar]]

**District:** The entire island is the Synod's domain

**Address:** The Spire of Tides, Thalassar Isle, Valdris Bay

### Campus Description
Thalassar rises from the churning waters of Valdris Bay like a dream of ancient power. The island is barely a mile across, dominated by the Spire of Tides—a tower of blue-white stone that seems to grow from the living rock. Around it cluster lesser buildings: dormitories, laboratories, the great Library of Waves, and the infamous Sanctum of Chains where dangerous practitioners are held. The air crackles with contained magic; wards shimmer visibly on stormy days. Students learn quickly that the island itself watches them—the very stones report to the Archmage.

### Architecture
**Style:** Ancient Elven with Human additions—flowing curves and crystalline structures from the original builders, practical stone additions from later ages

**Notable Features:** The Spire itself is over 500 feet tall, with no visible seams in its construction. Its summit is permanently wreathed in storm clouds that the Synod controls. The Library of Waves is partially underwater, accessible only to those with the proper enchantments.

**Age:** The original structures predate human presence on the continent; the Synod has occupied the site for over 1,200 years

### Grounds
**Campus Size:** Approximately 800 acres (the entire island)

**Notable Areas:**
- The Spire of Tides (administration, senior faculty, restricted archives)
- The Library of Waves (main library, partially submerged)
- The Proving Grounds (outdoor practice area for combat magic)
- The Sanctum of Chains (prison for dangerous mages)
- Student dormitories (four houses based on focus)
- The Garden of Whispers (meditation area, said to be haunted)
- The Docks (the only approved landing point)

## Administration

### Headmaster/Principal
**Name:** [[Archmage Vylara]]

**Title:** Archmage of the Synod, Keeper of the Tides

**Description:**
Vylara appears as a half-elf woman of indeterminate age, though records suggest she has led the Synod for over three centuries. Her hair is silver-white, worn long and unbound, and her eyes are the color of deep water—so dark blue they appear black unless the light catches them. She moves with the deliberate grace of someone who has learned patience through outliving everyone around her. Her voice is soft, almost gentle, which makes her pronouncements of judgment all the more chilling.

**Philosophy:** Magic is a loaded crossbow in the hands of a child. The Synod's duty is to ensure the child knows how to aim—or to take the crossbow away.

**Reputation:** Respected, feared, and utterly opaque. No one outside the inner circle knows what Vylara truly wants or believes. She has ruled through three attempted coups, two invasions, and one assassination that should have killed her.

**Secret:** Vylara has been in contact with the entities known as the Deep Ones for decades—not to serve them, but to understand them. She believes the only way to prevent their return is to know them intimately. This knowledge is slowly corrupting her judgment in ways even she doesn't recognize.

### Governing Body
**Name:** The Circle of Archmages

**Composition:** Seven archmages, including Vylara, each representing a major school of magic or Synod function

**Powers:** The Circle sets policy, approves curriculum changes, authorizes major operations, and can theoretically overrule the Archmage (this has never happened)

### Administrative Politics
The Circle maintains a careful balance of power. Vylara's authority is absolute in theory, but she rules by consensus, knowing that alienating the other archmages could destabilize the institution. The Master of Wards and Commander of Inquisitors have formed an informal alliance focused on security; the Master of Students and Ambassador often advocate for gentler approaches. The Master of the Library remains neutral, concerned only with knowledge. Grundir's Artificers provide the magical items that make all operations possible, giving him quiet leverage over everyone.

### Teaching Methods
**Approach:** Rigorous, dangerous, and effective. The Synod believes mages must understand the consequences of failure viscerally. Training includes controlled exposure to magical disasters, observation of restrained dark entities, and practice under genuinely dangerous conditions.

**Class Size:** Small—typically 8-12 students per instructor for practical work, larger lectures for theory

**Practical vs. Theoretical:** Roughly 40% theory, 60% practical application. The Synod produces working mages, not scholars.

### Faculty Politics
Rivalries, alliances, and conflicts:
- The Evocation and Abjuration departments have a centuries-old rivalry over resource allocation
- Necromancy instructors are barely tolerated; several faculty members refuse to acknowledge them
- A growing faction believes the Synod's restrictions are too harsh; they're watched closely
- Competition for apprentices from the most talented students is fierce and sometimes ugly

### Hiring
**Requirements:** Graduation from the Synod with honors, at least 20 years of field experience, recommendation from current faculty, passing the Crucible examination (a dangerous practical test)

**Process:** Candidates teach a guest lecture, submit to deep magical examination, and are voted on by the department and approved by the Circle

### Forbidden Studies
What is not taught here and why:
- **Soul Magic:** Manipulation of souls is absolutely forbidden; too close to the practices of the Shackled God's cult
- **True Necromancy:** Raising the dead in body is prohibited; theory-only study is permitted
- **Blood Magic:** The calling card of dark practitioners; any student caught experimenting is expelled
- **Summoning Without Binding:** Calling entities without proper containment is a capital offense
- **Chronomancy:** Time manipulation is unstable; the last practitioner erased herself from existence

### Practical Training
**Fieldwork:** Fourth-year students accompany Watchers on low-risk missions; fifth-years may join Inquisitor operations (observation only)

**Internships:** Promising students may serve at royal courts as junior advisors, under strict supervision

**Practical Exams:** Each year culminates in a practical examination that tests real-world application. Failure rates average 15%; failure can mean death in advanced years.

### Research
**Focus Areas:** Warding theory, detection of dark magic, safe containment of dangerous artifacts, understanding the Deep Ones (highly classified)

**Notable Projects:** The Grand Ward (a theoretical shield for an entire city), the Binding Concordance (cataloging all known summoning entities), Project Stillwater (a secret initiative related to the underwater entity beneath Thalassar)

### Selection Process
The Synod does not recruit; they collect. Watchers stationed throughout Valdoria identify children showing signs of magical talent—typically between ages 8 and 14. The family is informed that their child has been "chosen" and will be educated at no cost. Refusal is... strongly discouraged. Those who hide magical children risk Inquisitor visits. Once at Thalassar, students undergo extensive testing to determine their potential and appropriate track.

### Student Life
**Daily Schedule:**

- Morning: Theoretical classes
- Midday: Meal and free study
- Afternoon: Practical training
- Evening: Homework, research, monitored practice

**Living Arrangements:** Students live in one of four houses based on their specialty focus. Each house has its own dormitory, common room, and traditions.

**Meals:** Communal dining in the Great Hall; food is excellent (the Synod treats its students well in this regard)

**Free Time:** Limited. Students may access the Library, the Gardens, and designated practice areas. Leaving the island requires special permission.

## Graduation

### Requirements
**Years of Study:** Minimum 5 years; average 6-7; maximum 10 before mandatory dismissal

**Courses Required:** All core subjects, at least one specialization track

**Thesis/Project:** A major magical work demonstrating mastery—an original spell, enchanted item, or significant research contribution

## History

### Founding
The Arcane Synod was established in 742 RS, sixty years after the end of the devastating Mage Wars. A coalition of surviving archmages, horrified by what unregulated magic had wrought, agreed to create an institution that would ensure such catastrophe never happened again. They chose Thalassar—an abandoned elven fortress—as their headquarters, recognizing that its ancient wards provided both protection and symbolic weight.

### Golden Ages
Periods of greatest achievement:
- **The Age of Codification (800-900 RS):** The Synod established the magical laws that still govern practice today
- **The Great Ward Era (1100-1200 RS):** Synod mages created protective enchantments still shielding major cities
- **The Enlightened Period (1600-1700 RS):** Unprecedented magical discoveries, including stable portal creation

### Scandals
Notable controversies:
- **The Binding Incident (1050 RS):** A Circle member attempted to bind a demon lord; the resulting battle destroyed a wing of the Spire and killed thirty
- **The Northern Defection (1400s RS):** The student who became the Winter King escaped with stolen texts
- **The Purge of 1723 RS:** Overzealous Inquisitors executed suspected dark mages who were later proven innocent

### Evolution
The Synod has evolved from a loose council of archmages into a rigid institution. Early flexibility gave way to increasingly strict rules after each magical disaster. The current era sees the Synod at its most powerful—and most restrictive. Some argue this has prevented catastrophe; others say it has created a pressure cooker that will eventually explode.

### Current Era
The Synod faces unprecedented challenges. The Covenant of the Unveiled Truth grows stronger, practicing exactly the magic the Synod exists to suppress. The Winter King probes the north. And beneath Thalassar itself, something stirs in the Deep Well—something that has begun appearing in Vylara's dreams. The Archmage has quietly begun preparing for what she fears may be the Synod's final test.

## Secrets

1. **The Deep Ones:** Beneath Thalassar, in the waters the elves fled, ancient entities slumber. The original elven fortress was built not to protect against invasion from without, but to contain something within. The Synod discovered this truth and has spent centuries reinforcing the wards. The "water" that graduates drink at their commencement creates a magical link that allows the Synod to monitor them—but it also connects them to the things below.

2. **Vylara's Corruption:** The Archmage has communed with the Deep Ones for decades, believing she can learn to stop them permanently. This contact has subtly altered her thinking. Her decisions increasingly favor isolation and control—exactly what the entities want, as fear weakens the will.

3. **The Winter King's Truth:** The Winter King was not expelled for forbidden magic—he was expelled because he discovered the truth about the Deep Ones and demanded the Synod act openly. The Circle chose to silence him rather than reveal the danger. His hatred is born of betrayal, not mere ambition.

4. **The Rogue Network:** Not all graduates who "disappear" are dead or captured. A secret network of former Synod mages operates independently, believing the institution has become too rigid. They call themselves the Unbound, and they've been growing.

5. **The Prophecy of Tides:** A divination hidden in the deepest archive predicts that the Synod will fall when "the tide turns from the sea to the sky." The Circle has spent centuries trying to interpret—and prevent—this prophecy.

## Plot Hooks

1. **The Escaped Apprentice:** A talented student has fled the Spire with a forbidden tome—one that contains information about the Deep Ones. The Inquisitors want to silence her; she claims she's trying to warn the world. Which side do you believe?

2. **The Drowning Dreams:** People across the coast are experiencing the same nightmare—something vast and cold rising from deep water. The Synod dismisses it as mass hysteria, but one old mage knows better. The Deep Ones are waking.

3. **The Anti-Magic Riots:** A plague strikes a city where a Synod tower stands; the people blame the mages. As riots threaten to burn the tower, the party must evacuate innocents while investigating the plague's true source—which may indeed be magical, but not Synod magic.

4. **The Inquisitor's Doubt:** A senior Inquisitor approaches the party secretly. She's discovered evidence that the Synod has been executing innocent people and covering it up. She wants the truth exposed but fears for her life.

5. **The Northern Invasion:** The Winter King launches an assault, and his opening move is to breach Thalassar's wards from within. The traitor is a current student—but which one? The party must find them before the Spire falls.

6. **The Unbound:** A mysterious organization offers to train a party member in magic without Synod oversight. They claim to want magical freedom; the Synod claims they're dangerous radicals. Both might be right.

7. **The Deep Well:** Something is emerging from beneath Thalassar. The Archmage needs independent operators—not bound by the Oath—to descend and seal whatever is rising. Or destroy it. If they can.
